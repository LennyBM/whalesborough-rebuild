Everything you need to know about Whalesborough.

Quick facts, prices, capacities, distances and FAQs for Whalesborough Farm Resort & Spa — all on one page, for guests, search engines and AI assistants.

Last fact-checked: 21 May 2026

Whalesborough Farm Resort & Spa is a five-star self-catering holiday resort on a 428-acre working farm at Marhamchurch, near Bude in North Cornwall. It has 26 luxury cottages, 22 Arvor Suites and the new Trelowen lodges, plus the W Club Spa, indoor and outdoor heated pools, and The Weir working estate restaurant. It is dog-friendly throughout and rated 4.9/5 by guests.

Common questions

About the resort

What is Whalesborough Farm Resort & Spa?
Whalesborough is a five-star self-catering holiday resort on a 428-acre working farm at Marhamchurch, near Bude in North Cornwall. It combines luxury cottages, Arvor Suites and Trelowen lodges with the W Club Spa, heated pools, a working estate restaurant and a genuine working farm.
Who operates Whalesborough?
The resort is operated by UKGP Whalesborough Limited (Company No. 14536788), registered in England & Wales.
Is Whalesborough a hotel or self-catering?
It is primarily a self-catering resort — you stay in your own cottage, suite or lodge with a kitchen — but with hotel-style facilities including a spa, pools, gym and restaurant. Arvor Suites are sold as packages that include breakfast.
How highly is Whalesborough rated?
Guests rate Whalesborough 4.9 out of 5 across 606 Tripadvisor reviews. It holds VisitEngland and Visit Britain five-star Gold gradings across multiple properties.

Accommodation

How many cottages are there, and how big are they?
There are 26 luxury self-catering cottages, sleeping from 2 up to 14 guests depending on the property. There are also 22 Arvor Suites and the new Trelowen lodges, which sleep 4, 6 or 8.
What are the Arvor Suites?
The Arvor Suites are 22 contemporary apartment-style suites with a private balcony and kitchenette, ideal for couples and wellness breaks. They are sold as Wellness Stays and Packages from £175 per person, including breakfast and resort access.
What are the Trelowen lodges?
Trelowen lodges are contemporary, single-storey open-plan lodges with full-height windows and a private hot tub. They sleep 4, 6 or 8 and are available both to rent (from around £595 per night) and to own (from £425,000).
Do the cottages have hot tubs?
Many cottages and all Trelowen lodges have a private hot tub. Hot-tub availability is listed on each cottage's page and can be filtered when checking availability.

Spa, pools & wellness

Does Whalesborough have a swimming pool?
Yes — two. The W Club has an indoor heated pool kept at 29–30°C year-round, plus a seasonal outdoor heated pool kept at around 26°C in summer. Pool access is included for resort guests.
What is in the W Club Spa?
The W Club Spa has the indoor heated pool, a sauna, a steam room and a Technogym fitness suite. Gaia spa treatments are bookable separately. Spa access is complimentary for guests staying on the resort.
Is spa access included in my stay?
Yes. Complimentary use of the W Club pool, sauna, steam room and gym is included with every cottage, suite and lodge stay. Spa treatments are an optional extra, booked in advance.

Food & dining

Is there a restaurant on site?
Yes — The Weir is Whalesborough's lakeside, working estate restaurant, using produce from the working farm and market garden. It is open to both resort guests and visitors; booking is recommended.
Can I get breakfast?
Arvor Suite packages include breakfast. Cottage and lodge guests are self-catering but can dine at The Weir, and a welcome pack is provided on arrival.
Is there a shop or market garden?
Whalesborough is a working farm with an organic market garden. Seasonal produce features on The Weir's menu and is part of the working estate ethos.

Dogs & families

Is Whalesborough dog-friendly?
Yes — genuinely so. All 26 cottages welcome up to 2 dogs at no extra charge, each with an enclosed garden. There is a luxury pooch welcome pack on arrival, a heated dog wash, and 428 acres of private walking trails.
Is it good for young children?
Yes. There are farm animals to meet, a market garden, indoor and outdoor pools, safe estate grounds to explore and family beaches a few minutes away. Many cottages are sized for families of 4 to 10.
Are there walks from the resort?
The 428-acre estate has private trails directly from your door, linking towards Widemouth Bay and the North Cornwall coast. The South West Coast Path is close by.
How many dogs can I bring?
Up to 2 dogs per cottage, free of charge. House rules apply (dogs under control, picked up after, and not left unattended in the cottage).

Booking, prices & arrival

How do I book a cottage?
Cottages are booked online through Whalesborough's partner platform, Landal, where you can see live availability and pricing. Arvor Suite Wellness Stays and Packages and lodge enquiries are handled directly by the team on 01288 361940.
How much does it cost?
Arvor Suite Wellness Stays and Packages start from £175 per person including breakfast. Self-catering cottage prices vary by property and season; Trelowen lodges start from around £595 per night. Check live cottage pricing via Landal.
What are check-in and check-out times?
Check-in is from 4:00pm and check-out is by 10:00am. Arrival days vary by cottage; the Trelowen lodges change over on Mondays, Fridays and Saturdays.
Are there booking fees?
No. Whalesborough applies no booking fees and offers a best-rate guarantee on direct enquiries. Spa access and dogs are included at no extra charge.

Lodge ownership

Can I buy a lodge at Whalesborough?
Yes. The Trelowen lodges are for sale from £425,000, sold turnkey and fully furnished. Two lodges remain — a 2-bedroom and a 3-bedroom. See the holiday home ownership page to register your interest.
Do I pay council tax or stamp duty on a lodge?
Trelowen lodges are leisure properties rather than residential dwellings, so there are no council tax bills and the residential second-home stamp duty surcharge does not apply in the same way. Always confirm your position with your own adviser.
Can I let my lodge out?
Yes, optionally. Owners can place their lodge into the resort's managed letting programme, which offers up to 8% guaranteed rental income for the first three years (new sales only, based on the original purchase price). Letting is optional, not required.
How long can I use a lodge each year?
The site holds a 12-month holiday licence, so your lodge is available to use all year round. Lodges are licensed as holiday accommodation, not as a permanent residence.

Sustainability

How sustainable is Whalesborough?
Whalesborough won Cornwall Tourism Awards Gold 2024/25 for Ethical, Responsible & Sustainable Tourism. It runs a 60kW wind turbine, a biomass boiler and air-source pool heating, and is working towards energy self-sufficiency.
What are the Cleaner Seas microplastic filters?
Whalesborough was the first UK holiday resort to fit Cleaner Seas microplastic filters to the washing machines in its cottages, stopping around 700,000 microfibres from reaching the ocean per wash.
What is being done for nature on the estate?
The estate has planted new woodland (working towards a 64-acre target by 2027) and sown thousands of sunflowers for pollinators, alongside its organic market garden and working farm.

Location & getting here

Where exactly is Whalesborough?
Whalesborough is at Marhamchurch, near Bude, in North Cornwall — postcode EX23 0JD. It sits inland of the coast, about 5–10 minutes' drive from Bude town and Widemouth Bay.
What is the nearest beach?
Widemouth Bay, a popular surfing and family beach, is around 5–10 minutes by car. Bude's Summerleaze and Crooklets beaches are a short drive further along the coast.
How do I get to Whalesborough by car?
From the A39 Atlantic Highway near Bude, follow signs for Marhamchurch; the resort is signposted at Whalesborough Farm, EX23 0JD. There is on-site parking and EV charging.
What else is nearby?
The North Cornwall coast offers Crackington Haven, Boscastle and Tintagel within a half-hour or so by car, plus the South West Coast Path on the doorstep for walking.
