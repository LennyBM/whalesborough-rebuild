The Coast

Some of England's best beaches begin at your doorstep

Crooklets Beach

0.8 miles · Surf & Swim

Bude's most northerly surf beach. Wide, sandy, lifeguarded in summer. A steady beach break that suits beginners and intermediates. Consistent swell, good facilities, and a car park right above the sand.

Summerleaze Beach

1.2 miles · Town & Canal

Bude's town beach where the canal meets the sea. A unique tidal sea pool, the Bude Sea Pool, sits at the southern end — heated by the sun, sheltered from waves. A local institution.

Widemouth Bay

3 miles · Best Surf

The most popular surf beach in North Cornwall. A two-mile arc of golden sand with powerful, consistent waves that draw surfers from across the country. Excellent surf schools operate year-round.

Sandy Mouth

5 miles · Wild & Unspoilt

National Trust-owned cove tucked between dramatic cliffs. No development, no noise — just Atlantic rollers, rock pools, and a rare sense of wildness. Excellent for dogs and coastal walking.

South West Coast Path

630 miles of path.
The most dramatic part
runs through here.

The South West Coast Path runs along the clifftops above Bude, connecting Hartland Point to the north and Widemouth Bay to the south. The stretch through Morwenstow — just 6 miles up the coast — is regularly cited as the finest coastal scenery in England.

The estate's own network of footpaths connects directly to the Bude Canal towpath and Sustrans cycle tracks. You can leave Whalesborough on foot and walk to Bude, along the coast, or up to Morwenstow without touching a road.

  • 2 mi

    Estate to Bude via the Canal

    Flat, easy walking along the Bude Canal towpath into town. Excellent for dogs, pushchairs, and anyone wanting a gentle evening stroll to a pub.

  • 6 mi

    Bude to Morwenstow

    A classic half-day coastal route. North from Bude via Crooklets, Sandy Mouth and Duckpool to the remote hamlet of Morwenstow. Return via lanes or taxi.

  • 8 mi

    Bude to Widemouth Bay Loop

    South along the coast path to Widemouth Bay, then return via inland lanes. Dramatic cliff scenery and the chance to finish with a swim.

  • 4 mi

    Estate Estate Loop

    A circular walk through the Whalesborough estate — woodland, meadows, the working farm, and views over the Atlantic. Your dogs will be tired out by the third mile.

Days Out

Two hours in any direction and Cornwall astonishes

25 mi

Heritage

Tintagel Castle

The legendary birthplace of King Arthur, perched on a peninsula of sheer black cliffs above the Atlantic. English Heritage have created a breathtaking footbridge connecting the mainland to the castle ruins. Allow half a day.

30 mi

Coast & Village

Port Isaac

The most photogenic fishing village in Cornwall — familiar to many as Doc Martin's Portwenn. Narrow lanes, whitewashed cottages, a working harbour, and outstanding fresh fish. Arrive early in season.

45 mi

Food & Harbour

Padstow

Rick Stein's home port and one of the finest food towns in Britain. The harbour front, the Camel Trail for cycling, and the beaches at Trevone and Harlyn Bay make it a natural full-day destination.

18 mi

Heritage & Harbour

Boscastle

A medieval harbour village tucked into one of the most dramatic inlets on the North Cornish coast. The Museum of Witchcraft and Magic is genuinely fascinating. The walk in from the car park through the valley is stunning.

22 mi

Moor & Legend

Bodmin Moor

Wild granite moorland with Bronze Age monuments, ancient stone circles, and the highest point in Cornwall. Jamaica Inn sits at the heart of it. Rough Tor and Brown Willy offer magnificent walks on clear days.

14 mi

Market Town

Launceston

Cornwall's ancient capital with a Norman castle commanding views over the Tamar valley. Good independent shops, the Steam Railway, and a gateway to both Dartmoor and the Tamar Valley.

Bude Town

A surf town that never
takes itself too seriously.

Bude sits less than a mile from the estate. It has the energy of a proper surf town — board shops, wetsuit hire, excellent independent food — without the crowds of Newquay or the prices of Padstow.

The Bude Canal is one of the few remaining navigable canals in the South West. The towpath is flat, traffic-free, and connects Bude to Holsworthy — excellent for walking and cycling with children and dogs.

The Bude Sea Pool is a tidal open-air pool at Summerleaze Beach — free to use, warmed by the sun, sheltered from the Atlantic swell. One of the best wild swimming spots in the country.

Surf & Adventure

The Atlantic arrives here
with serious intent.

Bude sits on one of the most consistent stretches of surf in England. Crooklets and Widemouth Bay receive Atlantic swell year-round — summer is beginner-friendly, autumn and winter bring the serious waves.

Surfing

Multiple SASA-accredited surf schools operate at Crooklets and Widemouth Bay. Board and wetsuit hire widely available. Lessons for all ages.

Coasteering

Jump, swim and scramble along the cliff edges north of Bude. Guided sessions available for groups. Minimum age 8.

Kayaking & SUP

Paddle the Bude Canal for calm-water exploration, or take a guided sea kayak along the coast from Bude harbour to Millook or beyond.

Cycling

Sustrans cycle tracks connect directly from the estate. EV charging and secure bike storage available at Whalesborough. Hire available in Bude.
