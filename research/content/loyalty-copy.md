### The Manifesto

For three generations the same families have been driving down the lane in late summer, the boot full of dog and damp towels. They knew the shape of the orchard before the orchard knew them. They knew which lodge had the view of the lake and which seat in The Weir caught the last of the light. They came back, and they came back, and they came back — and nothing on paper ever marked that.

The Whalesborough Circle marks it.

The Circle is our way of recognising what's already true: that the people who come here once tend to come again. Not because we ask them to. Because the place has a habit of growing on them. The orchard in May. The lake in October. The sound of the rain on the hot-tub cover at midnight. These are small returns, and they compound.

So we built a simple programme that compounds in turn. Every pound spent — at The Weir, at the spa, on a lodge, on a wedge of cheese from the deli — earns a small currency we call Acorns. Acorns settle in your Wallet inside the app, alongside your tier ring, your next booking, your birthday note. They redeem one-to-one against another stay, another supper, another massage, another bottle from the wine list. Five percent back at Visitor, climbing to ten percent at Founder. No expiry until twenty-four months of silence. No blackout dates above Member tier. No catch.

There are five tiers — Visitor, Friend, Member, Patron, Founder — and you move through them by spending, slowly, the way you'd move through a long lunch. £500 lifts you to Friend. £2,500 to Member. £7,500 to Patron. £20,000 to Founder. Tiers are measured on a rolling twelve months, the way Hyatt does it, not the way the calendar does — so a Member in November is still a Member the following March. If you're within ninety percent of the next tier as the year closes, we round you up. If you slip a tier, we send a small voucher before we drop you. We don't believe in punishing loyalty for taking a quiet winter.

Acorns earn at a slightly different pace depending on how you book. Direct via the Whalesborough app earns 1.2x. OTAs earn 0.8x — Booking.com and Expedia take their pound of flesh, so we ask you to come to us first, and we hand a little back in return. Shoulder-season midweek stays earn triple, because that's when we love a quiet house most, and we'd rather reward you for choosing a Tuesday in February than chase you with a discount banner.

The Circle is free. It is automatic. It is the connective tissue underneath W Club spa membership, underneath Lakeside Locals, underneath lodge ownership — every paid member is also a Circle member, every Circle member is not necessarily paid. It is the simplest possible version of the relationship we already have with our regulars. We just wrote it down.

Welcome in.

### Acorns: how they work

**Earn rate.** 5 Acorns per £1 spent across the resort, multiplied by your tier. Visitor 1.0x. Friend 1.1x. Member 1.25x. Patron 1.5x. Founder 2.0x. At Founder, a £200 supper earns 2,000 Acorns. Two thousand Acorns is twenty pounds off your next bill.

**Redemption.** 100 Acorns equals £1, redeemable against accommodation, spa treatments, restaurant covers at The Weir (Friend tier and above), retail across the deli and wine list, gift cards for someone else, and a small curated list of Acorn Experiences — a hot-tub turndown at 500, a private breakfast in your lodge at 1,500, a couples massage upgrade at 5,000, a helicopter to dinner at 50,000 if you ever feel that way inclined.

**Channel matters.** Book direct through the Whalesborough app and you earn 1.2x. Book through an OTA and you earn 0.8x. The math: a £1,000 stay direct earns 1,200 Acorns. The same stay through Booking.com earns 800. Over a year of regular returns the difference is not small.

**Bonus events.** Triple Acorns on midweek stays Tuesday-to-Thursday in November through March, school holidays excluded. We are at our most beautiful when half-empty. This is how we say thank you for choosing the quiet.

**Expiry.** Acorns rest in your Wallet for 24 months from the last qualifying activity. Any earn or redemption resets the clock. We will warn you at 30 and 7 days before any Acorns are about to expire, so you have time to spend them on a good supper.

Life happens. A bereavement, a parental leave, a serious illness — these are not reasons to lose your status. ### Frequently asked

**Is the Circle really free?**
Yes. Free to join. Free to belong to. No annual fee. No joining fee. Free.

**Do Acorns earn on gift cards?**
On the recipient's transaction when they redeem, yes. Not the buyer's purchase. UK gift card law.

**Can I redeem Acorns against alcohol?**
Member tier and above, yes. Below Member, no — a UK licensing housekeeping note.

**What about tips and gratuities?**
Acorns don't earn on gratuities, and you can't redeem against them either. We'd rather your tip goes to the team in full.

**I lost my membership card from another club — can I still status match?**
A statement from the club, a current invoice, a screenshot of your active account dashboard — any of those work. Email **circle@whalesborough.co.uk**.

**What happens if I cancel a stay?**
Acorns earned on a stay are released only when the stay completes. Cancelled bookings reverse the Acorns. We're flexible about everything else — see the cancellation policy on the booking page.

### 2.1 /loyalty/visitor — Visitor

**Visitor.**
*Where the Circle begins.*

The moment you make your first booking — a supper at The Weir, a treatment at the spa, a single night in a lodge — you become a Visitor in the Whalesborough Circle. Nothing to sign. Nothing to pay. Just an opt-in box at checkout and a clean slate on your Wallet.

Visitor is the entry tier. It's free, it's automatic, and it does three things from the day you join:

**You start earning Acorns.** Five Acorns per pound spent across the resort, multiplied by Visitor's 1.0x base rate. That's 5% back, in the same currency, to spend on whatever you come back for. Stay a weekend, eat at The Weir on the Saturday night, walk away with the equivalent of a complimentary breakfast on your next visit.

**You get the welcome.** A handwritten note on the bed the first night you stay with us. A birthday voucher in your Wallet, every year, no exceptions. Push notifications and emails set to your preferences — and quiet hours by default, so we never wake you.

**You see the path.** Your Wallet shows a tier ring with a clear delta to Friend: spend £500 more, and the ring thickens. Friend unlocks the first real perks — 5% off room rates, 7-day early booking, 12pm checkout. Most Visitors who make a second stay are already Friend before they leave.

What Visitor is **not**: it isn't a loyalty card you have to remember. It isn't a points programme you need to game. It isn't dressed up to look richer than it is. Visitor is the cleanest baseline we could design — earn, see, climb if you want to. If you don't, your Acorns wait twenty-four months, then quietly expire.

**The one thing to know.** Book direct via the Whalesborough app and your earn rate climbs to 1.2x even at Visitor — that's 6% back from your very first booking. Book via Booking.com or Expedia and it drops to 0.8x. The cheapest direct rate is always on our app. We'd rather not pay the OTA commission, and we'd rather give it back to you instead.

### 2.2 /loyalty/friend — Friend

**Friend.**
*Earned in one good year.*

£500 across twelve rolling months. One short break, or four or five suppers, or a handful of spa treatments. The threshold isn't accidental — it's roughly the spend of a household that's chosen Whalesborough once and decided to choose us again.

Friend tier earns the same 5 Acorns per pound, multiplied by 1.1x. The maths: 5.5 Acorns per pound. A £1,000 stay earns 5,500 Acorns. That's £55 toward your next visit. Direct booking earns 1.2x on top, so the same stay through the app earns 6,600.

**What Friend unlocks:**

- **5% off room rates.** Applied automatically at checkout when you're signed in. Works on Best Available Rate; doesn't stack with sale prices.
- **7-day early booking.** Reservations for the busiest dates — bank holidays, late August, Christmas week — open to you a week before general release.
- **12pm late checkout.** Not subject to availability. Always 12pm. If you want to linger longer, just ask.
- **Welcome amenity.** A bottle of water and Cornish biscuits in your room on arrival.
- **Birthday voucher.** £10 in your Wallet, seven days before your birthday. Spendable on anything.
- **Redemption rights at The Weir.** Friend is the first tier where Acorns redeem against restaurant covers — not just retail and stays. A long Sunday lunch becomes a perfectly reasonable way to clear your balance.

**What Friend doesn't yet include**: spa retail discount, complimentary upgrades, anniversary gifts, the friends-and-family rate. Those start at Member. Friend is meant to be the first useful step — recognisable, not extravagant. If we made the welcome amenity any larger you'd never be able to climb to Member without feeling guilty about it.

The honest truth: most households who hit Friend in their first year hit Member by year two. The cadence of returning here is gentle but real. We see it in the data.

### Cancellation

In plain English:

### UK gift card rules — what you should know

We follow UK gift card law closely. The basics:

## 12. Loyalty Mechanics Explainer — Microcopy

A reference set for the small UI surfaces — tooltips, info popups, footer FAQs, in-app help sheets. Keep these in the voice that runs the rest of the programme: plain, specific, no marketing-speak.

---

### 12.1 What is an Acorn?

A small currency you earn across the resort and redeem against future visits. 100 Acorns = £1. Acorns earn on accommodation, spa treatments, restaurant covers, retail and gift cards. They don't earn on tips, gratuities, or third-party bookings.

### 12.2 How fast do Acorns earn?

5 Acorns per £1 at Visitor. Each tier multiplies the base rate: Friend 1.1x, Member 1.25x, Patron 1.5x, Founder 2.0x. Direct booking via the app earns 1.2x on top. OTAs earn 0.8x.

### 12.3 What can I spend Acorns on?

Accommodation (any room, any date — no blackouts from Member tier and above), spa treatments and day-spa entries (from Member tier; Friend tier redeems on retail only), restaurant covers at The Weir (from Friend tier), retail across the shop and deli, gift cards for someone else, and a curated list of Acorn Experiences (hot-tub turndown, private breakfast in your lodge, alpaca feed session, couples massage upgrade, helicopter to dinner).

### 12.4 What can't I spend Acorns on?

Third-party bookings (golf, off-site excursions), gratuities, alcohol on a dining bill below Member tier (a UK licensing detail). Service charges added to bills aren't Acorn-redeemable.

### 12.5 When do my Acorns expire?

24 months from the last qualifying transaction or redemption. Any earn or burn resets the 24-month clock. We send a warning email and a push 30 days and 7 days before any Acorns expire — there's always time to spend them.

### 12.6 What if I cancel a booking — do I keep the Acorns I earned?

Acorns earned on a booking are released only when the booking completes. If the booking cancels (yours or ours), the Acorns reverse out of your balance. Acorns spent on a booking that you cancel are returned to your balance.

### 12.7 Can I share my Acorns with someone else?

Not directly — Acorns are tied to your Circle account. But you can buy them a gift card with the spend, and they'll earn their own Acorns when they redeem it.

### 12.8 What happens to my Acorns if I close my Circle account?

Acorns are forfeit on account closure. We'll always write to you with a 30-day notice before any closure action, with a one-tap reactivation. If you want to use your Acorns first and then close the account, the support team can hold the closure until your balance clears.

### 12.9 How does tier review work?

Nightly. We sum your spend across the rolling previous 365 days. If you cross a threshold, your tier goes up automatically — sometimes you'll wake up to find you've climbed overnight. Tier review never moves you down by surprise: if you slip a tier, we send a win-back voucher of equivalent value first, then settle you at the new tier 30 days later.

### 12.10 What's the "soft-landing rule"?

If you're within 90% of the next tier's threshold at month 11 of your rolling year (i.e. close but not quite), we round you up automatically. We'd rather extend a small goodwill than have you miss a tier by £200.

### 12.11 Can I pause my Circle tier?

Yes — for life events: bereavement, parental leave, serious illness. Up to 12 months, concierge-administered, no fee. Tier-pause isn't self-service; message your concierge.

### 12.12 Are Acorns taxable as a benefit?

Acorns are loyalty currency, not cash. They are not taxable as a benefit-in-kind under HMRC guidance, since they're earned in the normal course of being a Whalesborough customer, not as employment compensation. (We are not your accountants — if you're using Acorns at scale through business expenses, ask yours.)

### 12.13 What does "direct booking" mean for the 1.2x multiplier?

Bookings made through:
- The Whalesborough app (iOS or Android)
- whalesborough.co.uk (when signed into your Circle account)
- Phone bookings handled by our reservations team

OTA bookings (Booking.com, Expedia, Mr & Mrs Smith, etc.) earn at the 0.8x rate.

### 12.14 Gift card Acorns — who earns them?

The recipient. When they redeem the card, they earn Acorns on the underlying transaction at their tier's rate. UK gift card law sees the redemption — not the purchase — as the loyalty-relevant transaction.

### 12.15 Triple Acorns on shoulder season — what counts?

Stays Tuesday through Thursday during November, January, February and March. School holidays excluded (UK England-and-Wales calendar). December excluded as well — too busy to subsidise. Two-night minimum to qualify for the multiplier.

### 12.16 What's the difference between W Club and Circle?

Circle is the free, automatic loyalty programme that sits underneath everything — every spend across the resort earns Acorns, tiers climb with cumulative spend, no fee. W Club is the paid spa membership — recurring monthly subscription, locker + facility access, treatment perks. Every W Club member is also a Circle member. Not every Circle member is in W Club.

### 12.17 What's the difference between Lakeside Locals and Circle?

Lakeside Locals is the £35-a-year restaurant membership focused on The Weir — 10% off year-round food bills, event priority, birthday dessert. Circle is the resort-wide loyalty programme. Lakeside Locals members are auto-enrolled at Friend tier in the Circle. The two stack and don't conflict.

### 12.18 Can I have a Circle account without ever staying?

Yes. You can have a Circle account from a single restaurant booking, a single spa treatment, even a gift card redemption. The minimum spend for a Visitor account is £1. Most accounts are created at the first booking (any kind).

### 12.19 What about kids and family Circle accounts?

Adult members hold a household Circle account. Children under 16 don't have separate accounts — their spend rolls into the household. From 16 they can have their own account if they want one, but spend across both accounts can be linked at request (we don't merge automatically because that creates GDPR complications).

### 12.20 GDPR and your Circle data — in plain English

We collect what we need to run the programme: name, email, phone, communication preferences, birthday, transaction history, Acorn balance. We never sell data to third parties. You can download your full Circle record at any time from your Wallet, and you can request deletion (right to be forgotten) — which closes the account and forfeits the Acorns. We hold transactional data for seven years for HMRC accounting compliance, even if your account is closed. The full privacy policy is at [/legal/privacy](/legal/privacy).
