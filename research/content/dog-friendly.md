### Editorial body (300 words)

There is a moment, usually around six o'clock on the second evening of a stay, when the dog stops checking. They stop checking the door. They stop checking the sofa for permission. They stop looking up every time a chair moves. They lie down by the log fire — wet from the lake or salted from the beach — and they fall properly asleep.

That moment is the whole point.

Whalesborough is a five-hundred-acre working farm in north Cornwall, on the lip of the coast at Marhamchurch. Twenty-seven of our cottages welcome dogs, which is to say every single self-catering cottage on the estate. Twenty-five of those have their own enclosed garden. Each comes with a soft-cover sofa throw, an indoor crate sized for a labrador, a dog towel by the door, and the standard Pooch Welcome Pack — bowl, beach towel, three treats, poo bags, walking map, branded bandana — set out on the kitchen counter with the dog's name on the card.

Outside, the estate is largely off-lead. We mark the on-lead stretches honestly — around the lambing fields in spring, the lake margin in nesting season, the kitchen-garden boundary — and we plot the rest as a quiet green wash on the map. There are eight water bowls scattered through the trails. There are dog showers at the main reception and at Cottage Cluster A.

The Weir, our restaurant, takes dogs at the bar, the snug, and the terrace. We will bring a sheepskin to the floor by your chair and a ceramic bowl of water without being asked. There is a three-item dog menu.

A short drive away there is Black Rock, off-lead and dog-friendly year-round. There is Sandymouth. There is Duckpool. We will tell you the truth about which beaches lock out for the summer, and when.

Welcome.

## 2. `/estate/dog-friendly/walks` — Walks spoke

### Intro (90 words)

A dog on holiday wants three things: somewhere to run, somewhere to drink, and somewhere quiet to fall asleep afterwards. We map the first two. The third happens by itself.

These ten routes cover the estate and the twenty-minute drive radius. Length, terrain, parking, and livestock notes are honest. Where we say "off-lead", we mean it; where we say "on-lead near livestock", we mean that, too. Mud levels update after rain. Tide-dependent routes flag the next safe window when you open them.

---

### W1. The Lake Loop — 1.4 km / 25 min — Easy — On-estate

**Off-lead:** Yes, except the western lake margin between March and July (ground-nesting birds).
**Water bowls:** Two — at the boathouse and the woodland gate.
**Parking:** Cottage car parks; no separate trailhead.

The shortest route on the estate, and the one most dogs do first. It begins at the boathouse, runs along the southern bank of the spring-fed lake, climbs gently through young oak woodland, and drops back through the kitchen garden hedge. The lake margin has a clean, shallow shelf where strong-recall dogs can swim — water tested monthly in season, lifejackets on loan at reception. In nesting season we lead-rope the western fifty metres; you'll see the soft amber wash on the map and a sign at the gate. Best as a wake-up walk before the kitchen garden gets busy.

---

### W2. The Forty-Five — 3.6 km / 50 min — Easy / Moderate — On-estate

**Off-lead:** Yes, throughout. On-lead through the alpaca paddock approach (50 m).
**Water bowls:** Three — boathouse, top gate, kitchen garden.
**Parking:** Cottage car parks.

Our "do this on a clear day" route. It loops from the lake, climbs to the headland on the east of the estate, and brings you back along the high field — full coastal panorama on the return leg, Bude visible to the south, Hartland Point on the horizon. Mostly grass and farm track. Two stiles, both with a low dog gate beside. Alberto and Alfonzo, the alpacas, live in the paddock you skirt; their reaction to dogs is studied indifference, but we lead-rope the fifty metres past the gate as a kindness to nervous dogs.

---

### W3. The Long Loop — 7.2 km / 90 min — Moderate — On-estate

**Off-lead:** Yes, with three on-lead stretches (lambing fields seasonal, lake margin spring, road crossing at Marhamchurch lane).
**Water bowls:** Five along the route.
**Parking:** Cottage car parks.

The full estate. Lake, woodland, headland, kitchen garden, river meadow, oak coppice, back to start. A good route for a young dog with energy to spend before lunch, or a slower walk for an older dog with the time to sniff. The river meadow stretch in May is the best ten minutes of the estate — bluebells, cuckoo flower, an early dog with the place to themselves. You will pass through Whalesborough's working farm — sheep are in the fields from October to April, lambs from late February to May. Lead-rope where the signs ask.

---

### W4. The Senior Sniff — 0.9 km / 30 min — Easy / Flat — On-estate

**Off-lead:** Yes, throughout.
**Water bowls:** Two.
**Parking:** Main reception.

Built for older dogs and joint-sore dogs. Flat throughout, soft grass underfoot, three benches placed at the right intervals for a human to sit and a dog to sniff. The route uses the lower farm tracks and the lakeside boardwalk — no stiles, no slopes, no fence-jumps. Pairs with The Long Sniff guided walk (Tue + Fri, 10:30, £8 — see Activities). The kitchen garden gate is closed during this loop so you don't have to recall a dog who's smelled the herb beds.

---

### W5. Black Rock & Widemouth South — 4.0 km / 60 min — Easy — 12 min drive

**Off-lead:** Yes, year-round, throughout.
**Water bowls:** One — at the car park tap.
**Parking:** Widemouth Bay south car park (free out of season, paid May–Sep).
**Tide:** Walk works at all tides; the southern reef is exposed at low and worth a look.

The nearest year-round dog beach. Black Rock sits at the south end of Widemouth — wide, flat sand, low cliffs, a reef at the far end. Off-lead, no seasonal restriction, no dog-ban signs. A dog can run for a kilometre in a straight line, which they rarely can elsewhere. Park at the south car park, follow the slipway down, walk south along the sand. Return the same way or, at low tide, scramble up the reef path back to the cliff. Beach shower at the slipway — free, cold, and very welcome. The Beach café back at the car park takes dogs everywhere.

---

### W6. Sandymouth Cliff Path — 5.5 km / 75 min — Moderate — 15 min drive

**Off-lead:** Yes, mostly. On-lead on the National Trust cliff edge stretch (200 m) and through the car park.
**Water bowls:** Yes, at the National Trust café.
**Parking:** Sandymouth National Trust car park (paid).

A South West Coast Path route that opens into the cleanest beach view in north Cornwall. Up from the car park onto the cliff, north towards Stowe Barton, drop down to Sandymouth via the National Trust path, walk the beach, come back along the shore at low tide or back over the cliff at high. Year-round dog-friendly. The cliff edge has a few sheer drops with no fence — call your dog in for those, especially with recall-trainee puppies. National Trust café at the car park takes dogs on the terrace.

---

### W7. Duckpool Valley — 3.2 km / 50 min — Easy / Moderate — 18 min drive

**Off-lead:** Yes, throughout.
**Water bowls:** One at the beach car park.
**Parking:** Duckpool car park (limited, ten spaces, NT honesty box).
**Tide:** Beach safer at low tide; cove is enclosed at high.

The quiet one. Duckpool is a small pebble-and-sand cove in a steep valley between Sandymouth and the Strangles. Most weekends you'll see four other people. Park in the valley car park, walk down to the beach for a swim or a paddle, then take the path up the south side along the stream — bluebells in April, foxgloves in June. The cove is dog-friendly year-round. Better for sandy-paw dogs who like a cold-water swim than for nervous dogs around surf.

---

### W8. Millook Valley — 4.1 km / 60 min — Moderate — 12 min drive

**Off-lead:** Yes, through the valley. On-lead on the lane back up.
**Water bowls:** None — bring a bottle.
**Parking:** Roadside at Millook (steep approach, large vehicles careful).

Millook is the geological wonder — folded slate cliffs the geologists travel to see. The beach itself is rough pebble, dog-friendly year-round. The walk goes from the beach back up the valley path, through ancient oak woodland (sessile oak, the Cornish coastal native), and returns on the lane. Damp underfoot most of the year. Good for a curious dog who likes a sniffy woodland; less good for a dog who needs space to gallop. Don't drive down if you're in anything bigger than a saloon — the descent is tight.

---

### W9. Bude Canal Towpath — 4.5 km / 60 min — Easy / Flat — 10 min drive

**Off-lead:** Mixed — towpath is on-lead by canal byelaw, but the marshland side is off-lead.
**Water bowls:** Several — Bude bowls dogs heavily.
**Parking:** Falcon Hotel car park or canal-side public parking.

A flat, easy route along the historic Bude Canal from the sea lock inland to Helebridge. The towpath is officially on-lead — heritage path, narrowboats, swans — but the parallel marsh trail on the far side is open access and off-lead. Bude is one of the most dog-welcoming towns in Cornwall: every café on the route takes dogs, the bookshop has a water bowl, the Falcon Hotel's beer garden is dog-throughout. Pair with brunch in town and the canal towpath either side of it.

---

### W10. The Strangles & High Cliff — 6.0 km / 100 min — Challenging — 25 min drive

**Off-lead:** Yes, throughout. Heavy recall expected — sheer cliffs.
**Water bowls:** None.
**Parking:** Trevigue car park (NT, paid).
**Tide:** Drop to the beach only at low tide; check the tide table on this page before driving.

The proper walk. High Cliff is the second-highest sea cliff in England, and the descent to the Strangles beach is the steepest path on the South West Coast Path between Bude and Boscastle. Off-lead throughout, but only for dogs with absolute recall — there are stretches where the path is a metre wide with a 200-metre drop to the sea. Stunning, lonely, unforgettable. Not the walk for a puppy or a leash-puller. We mention it because we get asked, and we think holding back would be patronising.

## 3. `/estate/dog-friendly/beaches` — Beaches spoke

### Intro (80 words)

Cornwall's beach dog rules are not uniform. Some beaches are year-round off-lead, no questions asked. Others lock out from Easter to October — and within "Easter to October" the exact dates change by council and by beach. We keep this page honest. The map shows you what's open today; the table shows you what's open for the season you're booking. The four year-round beaches are all under twenty minutes from the estate.

### Black Rock (Widemouth South) — 100 words

The default Whalesborough dog beach. Twelve minutes from the estate, year-round access, off-lead throughout, no zoning, no seasonal lockout. A wide tide-flat that goes for a kilometre at low water — the only beach in the local set where a recall-trained dog can run full-tilt in a straight line. The slipway has a cold-water rinse tap that works year-round. The Beach café back at the car park is dog-welcoming everywhere indoors, not just on the terrace. Surf school in summer at the north end of Widemouth, so the south end stays quieter even on August Saturdays. Park at Widemouth south.

---

### Sandymouth — 100 words

The cleanest sand in north Cornwall, in our reasonably partisan view. Year-round dog-friendly, off-lead, no seasonal restriction. Owned by the National Trust, which means the car park is paid but the trail is properly maintained and the café (dog-welcome terrace) is open most weekends out of season. At low tide the beach extends a long way north towards Northcott Mouth; at high tide it shrinks back to a steep band of pebble below the cliff. Good for a swim in summer if your dog handles surf; better in October when it's empty and the rock pools are full. Fifteen minutes from the estate.

---

### Duckpool — 100 words

The hidden one. A small cove between Sandymouth and the Strangles, set in a steep valley, reachable only down a narrow lane. Year-round off-lead. Pebble at the back, sand at low water, a stream running down the south side that becomes a paddling pool in summer. Most weekends you'll share the beach with four other people and seven other dogs. No facilities, no café, no shower. Park at the honesty box at the valley head — ten spaces, arrive early on bank holidays. Pairs well with the Duckpool Valley walk if you want to make a half-day of it. Eighteen minutes from the estate.

---

### Millook — 100 words

For the geologists, the foxes, and the dogs who like to think. Folded slate cliffs that earth-science students travel from Europe to see, with a small rough-pebble beach below. Year-round off-lead, no restriction. Underfoot is awkward — large, rounded stones, slippery when wet — so probably not the beach for a long-coated swim-loving spaniel. Better for the curious dog who wants to sniff seaweed and rock pools, then go inland for a proper run. The drive down is steep and tight; we wouldn't take a large vehicle. Twelve minutes from the estate, no facilities at the beach.

---

### Summerleaze, Bude — 100 words (seasonal)

Bude's main town beach, ten minutes from the estate, classic Cornish bucket-and-spade. **Dogs are banned from Good Friday to 30 September.** Out of season — October to Easter — the beach is open to dogs throughout, off-lead. The Tommy Jamieson Beach Café at the head of the beach is year-round dog-welcome on the terrace. There's a public dog shower at the slipway. The harbour and canal banks immediately south are open all year. Summerleaze in October at low tide with the sun setting is one of the better dog walks in Cornwall, and you'll have it largely to yourself.

---

### Crooklets, Bude — 100 words (seasonal)

The surf beach. North of Summerleaze, separated by a headland, also Bude town. **Dogs are banned from 1 April to 30 September.** Out of season — 1 October to 31 March — it's open and off-lead. In summer the surf school takes over the south end and the lifeguard zone takes the middle, so even off-season the south is busier than the north. Rosie's Kitchen above the beach is open most weekends with a dog-welcome terrace year-round. Pair with Northcott Mouth (off-lead year-round) on the South West Coast Path side if you want a longer summer walk that stays compliant.

---

### Widemouth Main — 100 words (seasonal)

The big sandy bay south of Bude. Surfers, kite-fliers, families, the Bay View Inn at the north end. **Dogs are banned on the main beach from Easter to 30 September**, with a permanent dog-friendly zone at the south end (which becomes Black Rock — see above). Out of season the entire bay is open and off-lead. In summer use the south end exclusively. The Bay View Inn is dog-throughout — they bring water without asking. Beach shower at the slipway. Thirteen minutes from the estate. The drive is the prettiest five-mile stretch of the Marhamchurch–Widemouth coast road.

---

### Crackington Haven — 100 words (seasonal)

A small valley village twenty minutes south on the coast road, the gateway to High Cliff and the Strangles. **Dogs are banned from the main beach Easter to 30 September**, but the rocks at the south end are open year-round and off-lead — there's space for a paddle and a stick-throw, just not the wide sand. The Coombe Barton Inn is dog-friendly in the bar all year. Pairs naturally with the High Cliff / Strangles walk for an ambitious day out — drive to Trevigue, do the walk, drop down to Crackington Haven for lunch.

## 4. `/estate/dog-friendly/features` — Features spoke (800 words)

### Intro (60 words)

Six things on the estate are designed specifically for dogs. The hardware — showers, crates, gardens, soft furnishings. The infrastructure — eight water bowls, two off-lead loops, the Long Sniff route. The welcome — a Pooch Welcome Pack on the kitchen counter with the dog's name on the card. We take them seriously because they're the reason 40% of our guests choose us.

---

### Dog showers — outdoor, warm-water, in three places (130 words)

The most-loved feature on the estate, and the cheapest. Three dog-shower stations: one by Main Reception (covered, year-round, warm water), one at Cottage Cluster A (the larger cottage group on the south side of the estate), and one at the spa entrance (for guests who walked the dogs on the way to a treatment).

Each has a thermostatic mixer set between 28°C and 35°C, a non-slip rubber mat, a tether ring at chest height, a microfibre towel hooked on the wall, and a body-wash dispenser of mild oatmeal shampoo. They are free, no booking needed, available from 7am to 9pm.

The single moment a wet, salted, sandy dog stops being a problem in the cottage and becomes a clean, content dog asleep by the fire — that's what the showers solve. Use them every time.

---

### Enclosed gardens — twenty-five of twenty-seven cottages (110 words)

Twenty-five of our twenty-seven dog-welcome cottages have a fully enclosed private garden — five-foot timber fencing, gated, no gaps. Two — Beachcombers and Westcotts — have shared garden access only; we flag this clearly on the cottage page and in your booking confirmation, and we offer either a discount or a different cottage if it's a deal-breaker.

A fenced garden does three things: it gives a dog somewhere to be without supervision for ten minutes while you make coffee; it gives an evening room with the door open while dinner is cooking; it makes mornings start at 7am with a tea, not a lead. We don't claim to be the only ones who have this. We are claiming to have thought about it.

---

### Indoor crates — every cottage, large enough for a labrador (100 words)

A heavy-duty wire crate sits in a quiet corner of every dog-welcome cottage — large enough for a labrador to stand and turn, with a fitted mattress and a folded blanket. It is there for two reasons: as a familiar quiet space for a dog who's used to one at home, and as a courtesy for guests who want to leave the dog briefly while they go to dinner at The Weir (which most guests don't, because dogs come — but the option is there).

If your dog is crate-averse, leave it folded against the wall. It's not in the way.

---

### Soft furnishings, fitted as if a dog lives there (110 words)

Every sofa has a heavy washable cover. Every armchair has a throw fitted at the seat. Bedrooms have a folded dog blanket on the chest at the foot of the bed for guests who let their dog sleep upstairs; downstairs cottages have one in the snug.

This is the bit nobody photographs but everyone notices. A dog-welcome cottage where you're nervous about the upholstery is not really a dog-welcome cottage. Ours are fitted to be slept on, dripped on, shed on, and stretched out on. The covers come off and go through the wash between every guest, which is also when the microplastic filter we fitted in 2023 to all machines does its work.

---

### Water bowls across the estate — eight, plotted on the map (90 words)

Eight ceramic dog bowls, each in a wooden frame, each sited in a place a dog would naturally want to drink — the boathouse jetty, the kitchen-garden gate, the top of the headland, the bench at the south end of the lake, the entrance to the spa lawn, the Weir terrace, the entrance to the Long Sniff loop, and outside reception. They are refilled twice daily by ground staff and after rain. Bowl locations are pinned in the Estate Navigator under Dog mode. In summer, all eight stay shaded by their siting; in winter, they stay free of ice with a small float.

---

### The Pooch Welcome Pack — what's in the standard included pack (140 words)

Every booked dog has a Pooch Welcome Pack waiting on the cottage kitchen counter when you arrive. The standard pack is included with every dog's stay — no surcharge, no opt-in. The contents:

- **A ceramic Whalesborough water bowl**, the same one used in The Weir, hand-thrown by a Truro potter
- **A natural-cotton beach towel**, sage stripe, large enough for a wet retriever
- **Three single-protein treats** — chicken, beef liver, sweet potato — packaged separately, labelled, all UK-sourced
- **A roll of compostable poo bags in a hessian dispenser** that hooks onto a lead
- **The walking map**, printed on linen card, with the ten estate-and-near routes
- **A branded cotton bandana** in sage

A card sits on top with the dog's name handwritten. *"Welcome, Bella. Your pack is on the counter; your towel is by the door; your bowl is on the floor."*

Five paid upgrade tiers are available — Premium, Allergy, Senior, Puppy, Travel — covered on the Welcome Pack page.

## 5. `/stay/dog-rules` — Rules page (1000 words)

### Intro (80 words)

We've tried to write these rules the way grown-ups would write them — short, specific, no euphemisms, no twee, no excessive disclaimers. They're the things that keep the estate working as a place where every dog can be a guest without any dog being a nuisance. Read them once before you arrive. If anything's unclear, the in-app chat goes straight to our duty manager, not a bot.

---

### Maximum dogs per cottage — by cottage size (90 words)

- **One-bedroom cottages** (Barley Park, Little Main, Middle Hill): **maximum 1 dog**.
- **Two- and three-bedroom cottages** (most of the inventory): **maximum 2 dogs**.
- **Four-bedroom-plus cottages** (Whalesborough Farmhouse, Whalesborough Cottage, larger managed cottages): **maximum 3 dogs**.

If you're travelling with more dogs than the listed maximum, contact us before booking. We've made exceptions for working dogs, registered assistance dogs, and well-behaved trios where the cottage layout supports it. The cap is about square metres of indoor space, not policy theatre.

---

### Age restrictions (60 words)

**Puppies welcome from 12 weeks**, fully vaccinated, with current vaccination card. We don't take un-housetrained dogs unless you flag it in advance and bring your own absorbent matting — we're not squeamish, but the housekeeping team needs the warning.

**No upper age limit.** We've welcomed dogs as old as seventeen. The Senior Welcome Pack, the Senior Sniff walk, and the Long Sniff guided walk are all built for them.

---

### Off-lead policy — what "off-lead" really means here (140 words)

Most of the five-hundred-acre estate is off-lead. We mean it. The route map shows what's on-lead and what's off, in good faith.

**Off-lead requires reliable recall.** If your dog has questionable recall, please keep them on a long line (a 10-metre training lead, which we'll loan from reception for free) until you know the estate. The Long Sniff walk is led by a member of ground staff and uses a fully enclosed route — perfect for the first day of a young or anxious dog's stay.

**On-lead near livestock.** Sheep are in the lower fields from October to April; lambs from late February to May. We rope-off and signpost these stretches clearly. Outside livestock weeks, the same fields are off-lead. Cattle are not on the estate.

**On-lead at the lake margin, March–July**, for ground-nesting birds. Signed and mapped.

---

### Cottage cleaning fee and standard (110 words)

There is **no per-night dog surcharge** on Whalesborough cottages. This is the policy difference we feel most strongly about.

A **flat dog cleaning fee** is added to every booking with a dog — £35 for one dog, £45 for two, £55 for three. This is one fee, not nightly, regardless of stay length. It covers the deep clean: sofa covers, throws, mattress protector if used, additional vacuum cycle.

This is not a profit centre; it is the cost of the housekeeping time. If you would like to know exactly where it goes, the breakdown is on the booking confirmation. We don't believe in hidden line items, and we don't believe in nightly per-dog surcharges. Cliveden charges £35 a night. The Ned charges £150. We do not.

---

### Off-lead-with-recall standard — what we expect of guests (90 words)

If you let your dog off-lead on the estate, the implicit deal is that you can recall them. If you can't, please use the long line until you can.

Dogs who chase sheep, jump on other guests without consent, or push their nose into another dog's space repeatedly will be asked, kindly, to be lead-roped for the rest of the stay. We say this because it has happened twice in the last year, both resolved cordially. Most dogs and most guests get it right without being told. We mention it because honesty is the policy.

---

### Things to bring (60 words)

- **A current vaccination card or digital record.** We don't routinely check, but we'll ask if there's an incident.
- **Your dog's regular food.** We can't take responsibility for diet changes during a stay. The kitchen at The Weir will keep your dog's food in cold storage if needed.
- **Any medication.** Note it on the dog profile.
- **A favourite blanket or toy from home.** Helps the first night.

---

### Dogs in the restaurant, the spa, the shop (90 words)

- **The Weir restaurant:** dogs welcome at the bar, the snug, and the terrace. Indoor bowls and a sheepskin floor mat brought to your table. Three-item dog menu — see /eat/dog-menu.
- **The W Club Spa:** dogs welcome on the spa lawn (a "quiet dog zone" outside the treatment building) but not inside the spa, the pools, or the treatment rooms. If your spa day is over three hours, the Day Den boarding service can take them — see Activities.
- **The Farm Shop and Reception:** dogs throughout, on a short lead, with water bowls at both doors.

---

### Third-party dog care during your stay (90 words)

If you've booked a long lunch, a spa day, or an off-estate day out where dogs can't come, the Day Den is a vetted kennel partnership we trust — drop-off model, half-day £45, full-day £75 including walk, lunch, two play sessions, and a photo report.

In-cottage dog-sitting is also available through the on-call concierge — £25 an hour, two-hour minimum, includes a walk. Both options are booked through the in-app chat. Our policy is that we never leave a dog alone in a cottage for more than four hours; we'd rather find them a person.

---

### Dogs and other guests (60 words)

A note on consent: please ask before letting your dog approach another dog or another family. Most guests at Whalesborough are dog-people; some have a nervous dog or a small child. The estate has space for everyone — eight off-lead acres per cottage if you spread them out — so there's never a need to crowd. Quiet greeting first, play second.

---

### What you'll be charged extra for (60 words)

- Damage beyond fair wear and tear (replacement of a sofa cushion or chewed leg)

**Their name** — *required*
This goes on the welcome card, the kitchen-side concierge sheet, and the daily arrivals list. We use names, not "your dog".

**Breed**
Type to search — Kennel Club list, plus "Crossbreed" and "Other" if it's a mystery. We use this only to size the welcome pack (a chihuahua's blanket is smaller than a wolfhound's).

**Date of birth**
We calculate age from this so we can auto-suggest the puppy pack (under 12 months) or the senior pack (over 8 years). No badge of honour either way.

**Weight**
A slider, kg. We use this to size the harness clip in the travel pack and to choose the right session length at the Pampered Pooch spa.

**Boy or girl, and neutered?**
Useful for the kennel partnership only — they keep entire dogs separate.

**A photo** — *strongly suggested*
For two reasons: the reception team can greet your dog by name and recognise them on arrival, and on return visits we use the photo on the daily arrivals list (no facial recognition database — just a human looking at a human-sized printout in the morning). Upload from your camera roll.

**Anything they're allergic to?**
Chips: chicken / beef / grain / dairy / peanut / custom. We use this to swap the welcome pack treats and to flag the kitchen at The Weir. If "custom", tell us in your own words.

**Anything they're on, medically?**
Free text. Encrypted at rest. Visible only to the duty manager and to St Mary's vet partner if you press the emergency button. We don't use this for marketing, ever.

**How would you describe them, in three words?**
Chips: anxious / dog-reactive / sound-sensitive / escape artist / loves water / loves people / doesn't like X. Or write your own.

**What do they like?**
Multi-select: long walks / water / fetch / sniffing / snoozing / meeting dogs / meeting people. We use this to suggest activities and the right welcome pack add-ons.

**How are they getting around these days?**
Chips: puppy / full mobility / slowing down / senior with joint support / registered assistance dog / wheelchair or cart. Built so we don't have to ask in person.

**Diet**
Kibble / wet / raw / mixed / homemade. The Weir kitchen will store your dog's food in cold storage during the stay if you're on raw — just tell us at check-in.

**Treats?**
Yes / no / limited only. Drives the welcome pack and whether we offer the dog menu at the table.

**Microchip number** — *optional, encrypted*
Stored encrypted. Used only if you press the in-app emergency vet button — we pre-load the chip to your clipboard so you can read it out to St Mary's without scrambling.

**Insurance provider and policy number** — *optional, encrypted*
Same logic as the chip. Surfaced only at the moment you need it, encrypted at rest.

**Vet practice name and phone** — *optional, encrypted*
The vet you use at home, for cases where St Mary's needs to speak to them directly.

**Emergency contact (a human, not you)** — *optional*
Only used in the rare case where you, the booker, can't be reached and a decision has to be made about the dog.

**Consent — can we feature Bella in the Dogs of Whalesborough gallery?**
Default OFF. Opt-in, withdrawable any time. We show a photo, a name, and the cottage they stayed in. Nothing else.

## 11. Dogs Trust partnership content (200 words)

**Why we work with Dogs Trust, and what 10% of the welcome pack revenue means.**

Whalesborough has been a Dogs Trust partner since 2022. The mechanism is straightforward: **ten per cent of every Pooch Welcome Pack sold — Standard, Premium, Allergy, Senior, Puppy, Travel — is donated to Dogs Trust** (the UK's largest dog welfare charity, founded 1891, no-kill policy for healthy dogs since the 1970s). The figure is audited by our accountant in November each year, and the year-end total is published on this page and announced in our newsletter.

We chose Dogs Trust over the alternatives for one reason: their **Hand It Over scheme**, which lets owners in real difficulty surrender a dog without judgement or destruction risk. That work needs unglamorous, steady funding. Welcome packs at Whalesborough are not, in any sense, a charity — they exist because guests want them — but the margin on them can be useful to a national charity in a way the margin on a Cornwall holiday cannot.

For 2026, the target is £8,000. For 2027, £12,000. The November announcement will be on the Dogs Trust partnership page, with a co-signed letter from their Cornwall regional manager.

## 13. No-surcharge policy — comparison microcontent block (100 words)

**On the policy difference.**

A note we put here because we get asked at least once a week.

There is no per-night dog surcharge on Whalesborough cottages. The flat dog cleaning fee (£35 for one dog, £45 for two, £55 for three — once, regardless of stay length) covers the housekeeping work. That is the only dog-specific line on the booking.

We point this out because the comparable UK luxury set has decided differently. Cliveden adds £35 a night. The Ned, £150 a night per dog. The Pig welcomes dogs at considerable extra cost and doesn't admit them to the dining room. We chose not to follow.

A dog is a guest of Whalesborough, not a permitted item, and we don't charge guests by the night for the privilege of being here.
