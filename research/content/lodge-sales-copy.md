6. **Where copy mentions income, the risk panel must be visually adjacent (sticky footer or end-of-section, never one click away).**

## What ownership actually changes

A residence at Whalesborough is, in practice, a permission slip. Permission to leave London at 4pm on a Friday because the lodge is ready and the dog is allowed. Permission to take three days off in October that you wouldn't otherwise have booked. Permission to have your in-laws and your sister's family in for Christmas without anyone sleeping on a pull-out sofa. Permission to do the surfing weekend in May because the swell is up and you don't need to fight Airbnb for it.

This is the case for ownership — not yield, not capital growth, not "investment opportunity". It's the small set of micro-decisions across a year that ownership makes easier.

---

## What you get that you can't get from booking

- **Continuity of place.** You know which bedroom the kids will be in, which mug you like, where the dog sleeps. By the third visit, the lodge feels like an extension of home rather than a holiday rental.
- **Off-season access.** February is one of the best months on the Cornwall coast for surfing and walking and there is more or less no demand. Owners use Whalesborough in February. Bookers tend not to.
- **Last-minute use.** Three-night trips because you can. No "minimum seven nights" rule applies to your own residence.
- **Dog welcome by default.** The whole estate is dog-friendly, but for owners there's no booking-window awkwardness about whether a particular plot allows pets.
- **The slow build of estate knowledge.** Which fields the bees are in. Which trail goes to the lake. The kitchen garden's seasonal rhythm. The barista at the spa cafe by name.

---

## The lifestyle inclusions that come with ownership

- Use of the W Club spa, indoor pool (29–30°C year-round), gym, sauna and outdoor pool (May–September)
- 20% owner discount on spa treatments and spa products
- Owner discount across The Weir restaurant and the spa cafe
- Walking and cycling trails across the 500-acre estate, the canal walk into Bude, the coast path at Widemouth Bay
- Farm-animal feeding days (donkeys, alpacas, lambs)

- Owner concierge — pre-arrival groceries, restaurant booking, spa booking, in-residence dining

---

## The honest constraints (the bit other sites leave out)

- The residence cannot be your main residence. Planning permission restricts the estate to holiday use only. If you need a Cornwall house, you need a Cornwall house — not a Whalesborough lodge.
- The 125-year licence reduces on resale. After thirty years of ownership, the residence sells onward with c. 95 years remaining. After fifty, c. 75. Buyers of short-licence lodges pay less than buyers of long-licence lodges.
- A residence at Whalesborough is a depreciating asset. The structure ages, fashions move, and resale prices may be lower than the price you paid. We do not market lodges as a vehicle for capital growth.
- Annual site fees are payable regardless of how often you visit and regardless of whether you let. Site fees fund estate maintenance, security, landscaping, road and path upkeep, the wind turbine, owner concierge.
- Most high-street lenders do not lend against lodge purchases. Bespoke holiday-home finance is available through specialist providers; we can signpost, but we don't arrange finance and we are not authorised to give financial advice.

---

## Who this is for (and who it isn't)

**Whalesborough ownership is a good fit if:**
- You have used Cornwall before and you know you want to come back regularly
- You have the means to fund the purchase without high-street mortgage finance
- You're comfortable that the residence may depreciate and you're not buying it for capital growth
- You want a second home that does the heavy lifting for you (spa, restaurant, farm, walks, dog-friendly) rather than a Cornwall house that you have to run yourself
- You're happy with the 11-month holiday-use restriction and have a primary residence elsewhere

**Whalesborough ownership is a poor fit if:**
- You're looking for a primary residence
- You're looking for a guaranteed-yield financial product
- You need high-street mortgage finance
- You want the ability to convert the residence to long-term residential lettings
- You'd rather have the flexibility of booking different Cornwall accommodation each year
