# Energy on the estate

**BLUF:** Whalesborough generates renewable energy onsite via a 60 kW wind turbine and a biomass district heating boiler that warms cottages and Arvor Suites via wood-chip. Pool heating uses air-source heat pumps. The estate publicly targets energy self-sufficiency; the original 2025 date has slipped, and the live dashboard shows the current honest self-sufficiency percentage rather than the target.

The 60 kW wind turbine is sited on the high ground at the back of the estate; its inverter publishes live kWh output to the in-app dashboard. Today's output, this month's output and the year-to-date are visible on the sustainability tile.

The biomass boiler is housed in a clean, well-lit plant room and burns sustainably sourced wood-chip from Cornwall mills. It provides district heating to the cottage cluster — radiators and underfloor heating run off it. The boiler displaces oil and gas that would otherwise be needed for heating.

Pool heating: air-source heat pumps move heat from outside air into the pool water. Far more efficient than direct electric resistance heating.

Honesty note: the publicly stated "fully energy self-sufficient by 2025" target was not met. Current site self-sufficiency varies by season — higher in windy autumn-winter months, lower in calm summer days when the turbine is quieter and demand is higher. The dashboard reports the live percentage rather than the missed target.
