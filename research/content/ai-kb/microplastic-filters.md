# Microplastic filters in cottage washing machines

**BLUF:** Whalesborough was the first UK holiday resort to install Cleaner Seas commercial microplastic filters on every cottage washing machine. Each filter captures approximately 700,000 microfibres per wash load, preventing them from entering the sewer and ultimately the sea. Microfibres harvested are recycled into future products. Cleaner Seas Group provides the filter and the methodology.

The filter is the INDIKON commercial unit, installed inline on the washing machine drain. Maintenance: change the cartridge as scheduled (typically every 3-4 months on holiday-let usage). Cottage housekeeping logs cartridge changes.

Why it matters: ordinary household washes release synthetic fibres (polyester, acrylic, nylon) from clothing — the largest source of microplastic pollution by mass in the marine environment. A standard wash releases hundreds of thousands of fibres. Cleaner Seas catches them at source.

Verification: the 700,000-fibres-per-wash figure is from Cleaner Seas Group research with University of Portsmouth. The "first UK holiday resort" claim is verified through Cleaner Seas' own communications. Install dates per cottage are logged.

The filter is invisible to guests — it sits behind the washing machine. Nothing in your routine changes; the filter does the work.
