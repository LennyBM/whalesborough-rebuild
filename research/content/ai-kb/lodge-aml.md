# Anti-money-laundering checks (AML) for lodge purchases

**BLUF:** Anti-money-laundering checks are a legal requirement on all property transactions in the UK under the Money Laundering Regulations 2017 (as amended). Whalesborough's solicitors complete identity verification on the buyer and a source-of-funds review before exchange. The process is standard, professional and usually takes 5-15 working days.

What is checked:

- Identity verification (passport or driving licence, current address proof — utility bill or bank statement within 3 months)
- Source of funds verification (where the purchase money has come from: savings, sale of a previous property, inheritance, business proceeds, loan)
- Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening
- Sanctions screening

What is needed from the buyer: documents listed above, plus bank statements or other written evidence showing the origin of the purchase funds. For inherited or gifted funds, supporting documentation (probate, gift letter).

The reservation deposit is taken before AML checks complete. The contract exchange does not proceed until checks are cleared. If checks raise a concern, the solicitors are obliged to investigate and may notify the National Crime Agency — this is a legal duty, not a discretionary judgment.

Aurelia notes: AML is mandatory and non-negotiable. Routed to the human sales team and the buyer's solicitor for any specific questions about the process.
