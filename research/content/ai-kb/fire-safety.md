# Fire safety at Whalesborough

**BLUF:** Every cottage and Arvor Suite has interlinked smoke alarms on every floor, heat detectors in kitchens and CO detectors where solid-fuel or gas appliances are fitted, in line with UK self-catering regulations. Each property has a documented fire risk assessment and a fire-action notice by the entrance.

If an alarm sounds in your accommodation, do not investigate — leave the building by the nearest exit, do not stop to collect belongings, and assemble at the parking area for your cottage row or the main car park near the spa. Call 999 first, then notify reception on 01288 361940. The estate has its own water mains and the property is registered with Cornwall Fire and Rescue.

Each cottage with a woodburner has a fire extinguisher and fire blanket in the kitchen, plus a hearth-guard and instructions for safe use. Always close the woodburner door before leaving the room. Do not place anything on or against the stove.

Disposable barbecues, sky lanterns, fireworks and bonfires are prohibited on the estate. Open-fire pits are permitted only at properties where one is installed and only with the wood provided.

If the alarm in your cottage chirps (low battery warning), call reception — do not remove the alarm.
