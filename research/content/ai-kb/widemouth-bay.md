# Widemouth Bay

**BLUF:** Widemouth Bay is a wide sand and shingle beach about 5 minutes' drive (and a 25-minute estate walk) south of Whalesborough. The bay is wide, the surf is consistent, the views are outstanding. Surf schools, beach cafes, year-round dog-friendly at the north end (south end seasonal).

The beach: roughly a mile long, sand at low tide with rocky outcrops, shingle at the back of the beach. Surfable on most swells with the south-west prevailing wind, sheltered at the north end on south-westerlies. Two car parks (north and south) and several lay-by stops along the coast road.

Surf: one of the most popular surf spots in north Cornwall. Suited to beginners through to intermediate; the south end has the more consistent wave. Several surf schools operate here (see surf-schools chunk).

Cafes and refreshments: The Black Rock Cafe (north end, dog-friendly), Widemouth Manor (further south, full menu), the surf-school cafes for coffee and a bacon roll. Public toilets at both car parks.

Dogs: north end (around the main car park) is year-round dog-friendly. The south end (around the surf school zone) is restricted October-May (dogs on lead) and dogs not allowed during the main summer season.

Tides: low tide reveals the rock pools; high tide makes for easier swimming. Check the in-app tide tile.
