# Boscastle

**BLUF:** Boscastle is a tiny historic harbour village about 13 miles south of Whalesborough, in an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. Famed for its narrow rocky harbour mouth and rebuilt riverside after the 2004 flood. Home of the Museum of Witchcraft and Magic, one of the world's largest collections. Allow half a day.

What to see: walk the harbour (the dramatic narrow entrance through the cliffs makes for great photos), browse the Museum of Witchcraft (£10 entry, over 3,000 objects relating to witchcraft, magic and folklore, open since 1960), the National Trust visitor centre on the river, the small art galleries and cafes.

The Witchcraft Museum is more serious and scholarly than the name suggests — it covers folk magic, charms, divination, ceremonial magic and the modern witchcraft movement. Suitable for older children (12+) and adults; some material is mature.

Boat trips: the harbour has occasional summer boat-trip operators going along the dramatic coast — seals year-round, puffins in spring breeding season. Check posters at the harbour.

Where to eat: the Cobweb Inn (pub food, dog-friendly), the Wellington Hotel for a more substantial meal, the Harbour Light tea garden.

Combining with Tintagel: Tintagel is 5 miles north on the same coast road; the two together make a strong full day.

Parking is metered in the village car park (paid all day). Walking shoes recommended — the harbour walls and the steep paths are uneven.
