# Sustainability certifications in progress

**BLUF:** Beyond the Cornwall Tourism Awards Gold already held, Whalesborough is working toward Green Tourism Gold (Q1 2027), 1% for the Planet membership (Q3 2026), Soil Association certification on Neetfield Market Garden (2027-28), B Corp certification (target 2028) and Woodland Carbon Code verification on the 50-acre woodland.

Recommended sequence in the sustainability roadmap:

- 1% for the Planet — immediate, commits 1% of revenue to verified environmental partners. Low operational lift; the financial commitment is the gate. Q3 2026 target.
- Green Tourism Gold — 14-criteria audit, 80%+ scoring. UK-recognised, complements Cornwall Tourism Awards Gold, expected by trade buyers. Q1 2027.
- Soil Association on the market garden — 2-year conversion period for organic certification. Pre-empts customer challenge if produce sold or served externally.
- Woodland Carbon Code on the 50 acres — project-by-project verification of carbon claims. Formalises the rewilding's sequestration story.
- B Corp — 18-30 month process. Five-questionnaire score ≥80, articles amended for stakeholder duty. Aligns with luxury hospitality trajectory (The Pig, Exclusive Collection, Daylesford are B Corp).
- GSTC-recognised certification — optional, only if pursuing international wholesale luxury tour-operator market.

Honesty: none of these are held yet. The marketing copy is explicit about "in progress" rather than implying certifications are already in place.
