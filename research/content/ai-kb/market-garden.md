# Neetfield Market Garden

**BLUF:** Neetfield is the estate's organic market garden — around 1 acre of raised beds, polytunnels and pollinator strips. No-dig method, compost-mulch top dressing, on-site composting cycle, seed-saving programme. Supplies The Weir Restaurant year-round and welcome hampers seasonally.

The garden is named for the field it sits in. The head gardener works it full-time spring-autumn and switches focus to composting, planning and cover-crop management in winter.

What's grown: leafy greens (kale, chard, spinach, lettuces), brassicas (sprouting broccoli, kalettes, cabbages), root vegetables (carrots, beetroot, parsnips, celeriac), alliums (onions, leeks, garlic), legumes (broad beans, runner beans, French beans, peas), tomatoes (in the polytunnel), cucumbers, courgettes, edible flowers, herbs (rosemary, thyme, sage, oregano, parsley, coriander, basil, lemon balm, mint), soft fruit (raspberries, strawberries, currants), and the famous autumn pumpkin patch.

No-dig method: the soil is never turned. Compost mulch is applied annually; weeds are pulled or smothered. The soil structure improves over time, water retention is better, weeding is reduced.

Open to guests for free Wednesday tours (see Market Garden Tours chunk). The gardener answers questions; tastings are common. Soil Association organic certification is on the roadmap (planned 2027).
