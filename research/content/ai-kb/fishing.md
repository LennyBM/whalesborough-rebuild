# Fishing at the lake

**BLUF:** Whalesborough has a well-stocked coarse fishing lake on the estate. Day-tickets for in-stay guests are £5 per rod per day; family day-tickets £15. Carry-along tackle is not provided; bring your own or borrow from a Bude tackle shop. Catch and release for all species.

Stocked species: carp (mirror, common and ghost), tench, roach, rudd, bream, perch. Carp to mid-20s lb have been caught. The lake is approximately 1.5 acres with a mix of bay, central island and reed margins.

Permit handling: get a ticket at reception or via the in-app activities tab. Children under 12 must be accompanied by an adult permit-holder; their fishing is free. A short briefing covers the catch-and-release protocol, unhooking mats and net hygiene.

What's permitted: barbless or micro-barbed hooks only; landing nets compulsory; unhooking mats compulsory for any carp over 8 lb; no overnight sessions without prior booking; no boilies with red dye; no bait fish.

What's not: keepnets, sacks, fish stress-treatment products outside the briefing list. No swimming, no boating.

Rod licences: you need a current Environment Agency rod licence (age 13+). Available online at gov.uk; £6 day, £30 annual.
