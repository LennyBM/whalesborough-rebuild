# Rewilding Whalesborough

**BLUF:** Rewilding at Whalesborough is the active programme of returning a working farm to a more biodiverse mosaic — woodland (50 acres newly planted), wildflower meadows (multiple acres), pollinator strips (9,000 sunflowers, 22,000 pumpkin seeds), pond restoration, hedgerow extension, and reduced-intensity grazing on remaining pasture. Bird boxes and bee hives integrated.

This is rewilding done in the British way — not a fenced wilderness with reintroduced apex predators, but a working landscape that gives more space, food and shelter to native species. Hedgerows become habitat corridors. Field margins become forage strips. Ponds are de-silted and re-planted with native marginals.

Key visible features for guests: the wildflower meadow in full bloom (June-July), the sunflower field (late summer), the apple orchard and bee meadow, the new woodland edges, the pumpkin patch (autumn).

Counts:

- 50 acres new woodland (verified)
- 9,000 sunflowers (planting records held)
- 22,000 pumpkin seeds (seed invoices verifiable)
- 8 active beehives, ~15,000 bees (verified, dashboard updated)

Partners: Wildlife Trust (accreditation), Woodland Trust (planting records), DEFRA recognition for the wider biodiversity work.

The estate avoids absolute claims ("rewilded" — past-tense and finished). Rewilding is in progress; the landscape will look different in 20 years.
