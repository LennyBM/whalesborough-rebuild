# Alpaca feeding sessions

**BLUF:** Whalesborough has resident alpacas (Alberto and Alfonzo are the named pair on the social channels). They feature in the twice-weekly Little Farmers feeding sessions. Daily location varies — alpacas roam the paddocks around the apple orchard, the upper field and the woodland edge. Standard public feeding times are 11:00 and 16:00 depending on the season.

Little Farmers sessions run twice a week year-round (typically Tuesday and Saturday at 10:30) and are free for in-stay guests. Sign up in the app — capacity around 15 per session. Sessions last 30-40 minutes and cover the alpacas, goats, pigs, chickens, ducks and the donkeys. A farm hand leads.

For self-guided alpaca visiting: the paddocks have public-side perimeter fencing and the alpacas come up to the fence for greetings. Take a moment, do not climb the fence, and do not feed them outside Little Farmers times — they get a controlled diet and outside food upsets it.

Tractor rides follow the summer feeding sessions and are weather-dependent.

In V1 of the app, "Alberto last spotted at 9:42am near the apple orchard" comes from the alpaca-cam and a lightweight tracker. For MVP, just head down to the paddock.
