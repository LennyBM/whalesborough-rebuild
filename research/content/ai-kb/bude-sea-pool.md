# Bude Sea Pool

**BLUF:** Bude Sea Pool is a free, semi-natural saltwater tidal pool at Summerleaze Beach in Bude, refilled twice daily by the sea. Open in daylight hours all year. Approximately 91 metres long, depth 4-6 feet at high tide. Lifeguarded in summer; swim-at-your-own-risk in winter. About 10 minutes' drive from the estate.

The pool is one of Cornwall's most loved free swimming spots. Built in 1930 against the cliff at Summerleaze, it allows safe sea swimming whatever the surf is doing outside. The tides refresh the water naturally — at high tide it can overtop and spill back to sea.

Sea temperature follows the open sea: 16-18°C in late summer, 9-12°C in winter. Cold-water swimmers love the winter sessions. A short wetsuit or rash vest is sensible year-round; locals often go skins.

Facilities at Summerleaze: car park (£4 for 4 hours typical), changing room (open in summer), public toilets, a small cafe, lifeguard hut. Dog rules vary by season; from October to mid-May dogs are welcome.

Access for less mobile guests: the path down to the pool is steep in places; the wheelchair-accessible route is via the slipway from the car park (gentler gradient). The pool itself has steps and rough rocks; assistance is recommended.

Cold-water acclimatisation: never enter cold water alone, build up gradually, exit within 5-10 minutes for cold-shock safety.
