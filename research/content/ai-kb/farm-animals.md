# Farm animals at Whalesborough

**BLUF:** Whalesborough is a working farm with alpacas, goats, pigs, sheep, chickens, ducks and donkeys. Animals are visible across the estate; the Little Farmers feeding sessions (Tuesday and Saturday 10:30) bring everyone to one feeding paddock. Self-guided visits are welcome — please do not feed outside organised sessions.

The cast:

- Alpacas — Alberto and Alfonzo are the named pair on social channels. Curious, gentle, will come up to the fence
- Goats — small group, mischievous, popular at feeding time
- Pigs — pasture-raised, in the lower fields
- Sheep — graze the rewilded pasture; please give them space and dogs on lead near paddocks
- Chickens — free-range, eggs to The Weir kitchen and the welcome hampers
- Ducks — on the lake, year-round residents
- Donkeys — the seasoned pair, gentle with children at feeding sessions

Welfare matters: do not chase animals, do not climb fences, do not feed outside organised sessions (the farm hand manages their diet), do not approach a mother and young, keep dogs on lead near paddocks (the working farm is not a dog playground).

Photography is fine — please share #whalesborough rather than scrape from someone else's account.

For deeper engagement: book the Beekeeper for a Day, the Market Garden Tour, or the Sustainability Tour for guided visits to the working spaces.
