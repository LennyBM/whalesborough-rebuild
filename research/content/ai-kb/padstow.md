# Padstow

**BLUF:** Padstow is a working fishing port at the head of the Camel Estuary about an hour's drive south of Whalesborough. Famed for Rick Stein's Seafood Restaurant and the cluster of related Stein establishments. Start point of the Camel Cycle Trail. Busy in summer; charming year-round. Allow a full day.

What to do: walk the harbour and the small streets, visit Padstow Lobster Hatchery (small charge, child-friendly), take the small foot ferry across to Rock for a beach walk (£3-5 return), hire bikes for the Camel Trail (18 miles to Bodmin), browse the independent food and gift shops.

Eating in Padstow: Stein's Seafood Restaurant is the destination but books months ahead; Stein's Fish & Chips on the harbour (no booking, queues at lunch), Rick Stein's Cafe, Paul Ainsworth's No. 6 (Michelin-starred, books well ahead), the many smaller cafes and bistros around the harbour. The Cornish Arms (Stein's pub) is in St Merryn, a 10-minute drive.

Parking: paid car park at the top of the town; can be busy summer weekends.

The Camel Trail: easy 18-mile flat cycle path along the old railway line; Padstow to Wadebridge is a popular 5-mile each-way ride suitable for families. Bike hire on the harbour.

Worth combining with: a beach day at one of the Camel beaches (Daymer Bay, Polzeath, Constantine).

Travel time from the estate: just under an hour by car. Not a quick visit.
