# Paddock-to-plate philosophy

**BLUF:** "Paddock-to-plate" is The Weir's working philosophy: 90% of the food and drink that arrives at your table is from the estate or a 5-10 mile radius. Restaurant copy commits to "fresh & mouth-watering flavors from paddock to plate." Provenance is visible on the menu — every key ingredient names its source.

What this looks like in practice: the eggs in your morning English came from chickens you saw at the Little Farmers feeding the day before. The salad on your lunch was picked at Neetfield Market Garden that morning. The fish was landed at Bude or Padstow harbour overnight. Bread was baked at a Bude bakery hours before service. Honey came from the 15,000 Cornish Black Bees in the hives at the top of the estate.

Why it matters: it's the right way to eat — fresher, tastier, lower-carbon than equivalent meals built on a long supply chain. The kitchen partners directly with named producers, paying fairly and committing to seasonal menus rather than year-round constants.

What we don't do: pretend to grow everything ourselves. Coffee, citrus, spices and some specialist ingredients come from further afield. We name the source on the menu rather than hide it.

Methodology for the 90% figure: weighted by spend across the menu, not a count of ingredients. Audited annually. The methodology is shared on request.
