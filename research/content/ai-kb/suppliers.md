# Restaurant suppliers and sourcing

**BLUF:** 90% of food and drink at The Weir comes from the estate or within a 5-10 mile radius. Eggs and seasonal vegetables from Whalesborough's own farm and Neetfield Market Garden; fish from sustainable day-boats out of Bude and Padstow; meat from named Cornish butchers; bread from a Bude sourdough bakery; coffee from a Cornish roaster.

Key suppliers (subject to seasonal change):

- Eggs — Whalesborough estate hens
- Salad, vegetables, herbs — Neetfield Market Garden (on estate, organic)
- Fish — Bude and Padstow day-boats (line-caught, sustainable species)
- Beef — local Cornish dry-aged from a named family farm in Stratton
- Lamb — Whalesborough estate (limited; supplements seasonal availability)
- Pork and bacon — Cornish pasture-raised
- Bread — sourdough from a Bude bakery (named on the menu)
- Cheese — Cornish cheesemakers (Cornish Brie, Cornish Yarg, Cornish Blue)
- Wine — focus on Cornish and English (Camel Valley, Trevibban Mill)
- Cider and beer — Cornish craft brewers (Sharp's, St Austell, smaller local breweries)
- Coffee — Cornish roastery (Yallah Coffee or similar)
- Spirits — Cornish gin (white-label or named Cornish distillery; the resort's own labelled spirits require verification — see related note)

A printable monthly supplier register is available on request and rotates seasonally with what is actually being served.
