# Sustainability at Whalesborough — overview

**BLUF:** Whalesborough holds Cornwall Tourism Awards Gold 2024/25 for Ethical, Responsible & Sustainable Tourism. Concrete credentials: first UK holiday resort with Cleaner Seas microplastic filters on every cottage washing machine; 60 kW wind turbine; 50 acres of newly planted woodland; biomass district heating; air-source pool heating; organic market garden; ~15,000 Cornish Black Bees in 8 hives; 9,000 sunflowers planted for pollinators; 22,000 pumpkin seeds; Ocean Saver plastic-free cleaning in every welcome pack.

The principle: every claim shows its working. The on-app live dashboard surfaces real numbers (turbine kWh today, microfibres captured this week, current hive count) rather than vague "we love nature" statements. Where targets have slipped — the publicly stated "energy self-sufficient by 2025" was not met — the honest current percentage is shown rather than the missed target.

Working partners: Cleaner Seas Group, Wildlife Trust (Cornwall), Sustrans, Ocean Saver, Gaia Natural Skincare, DEFRA (recognition under named scheme).

In-stay impact: at end of stay, a personalised "Your Impact" card shows the renewable energy used during your stay, the microfibres prevented from leaving the laundry, the food miles avoided, the woodland contributed via the optional Whalesborough Woodland Fund levy (£2/guest/night opt-in).

Aurelia notes: when discussing the "super-fast EV chargers" or "energy self-sufficient by 2025," she uses the verified language — rapid AC chargers (kW pending verification), targeting energy self-sufficiency with current status visible in the live dashboard.
