# Woodland and rewilding

**BLUF:** Whalesborough has planted 50 acres of new woodland on the estate as part of its rewilding programme. Mixed native species (oak, hazel, blackthorn, hawthorn, rowan, willow, alder), planted in stages from 2020 onwards. Verified via Woodland Trust / Forestry Commission records.

The planting plan deliberately mixes species to support biodiversity rather than monoculture. Edge habitat for birds and small mammals; closed canopy will develop over decades. Trees are protected with biodegradable guards.

What this adds: enhanced biodiversity corridor connecting estate to existing hedgerow networks, carbon sequestration (verified rates under the Woodland Carbon Code), habitat for insects, bats, birds and bees, shelter for the working farm landscape.

Adjacent ground: 9,000 sunflowers planted for pollinators and a striking summer field; 22,000 pumpkin seeds for the autumn patch. Wildflower meadow strips for the bees.

Estate-wide stewardship: 450 acres total. The 50 acres of new woodland is one programme within a larger picture that includes pasture rewilding, hedgerow management, pond and wetland restoration.

Verification: planting records held in the estate office. GPS polygons available on request. Annual ecological survey covers species count.
