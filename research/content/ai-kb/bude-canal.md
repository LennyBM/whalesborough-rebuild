# Bude Canal towpath

**BLUF:** Bude Canal is a 19th-century inland waterway whose towpath runs from Whalesborough's western boundary north to Bude town centre. About 3 miles. Flat, smooth path. Suitable for buggies, mobility scooters and family cycles. Dogs welcome year-round. The estate has a direct access gate onto the canal.

The walk from the estate to Bude town along the canal is one of the most popular options for guests — 50 minutes of easy walking with the canal on one side and fields on the other, finishing in town for lunch or coffee.

Wildlife: kingfishers, herons, dippers, ducks, the occasional otter. Reed beds along the canal margin. Best wildlife watching is early morning.

Cycling: the path is suitable for hybrid and family bikes; not technical mountain biking. Bike hire from a Bude operator delivered to the estate. The Sustrans cycle network connects from the canal to Bude town and on toward the coast.

History: the canal is 19th-century, built to bring lime sand from the beach inland for agricultural use. Restored by volunteers and Cornwall Council. The pump house and lock gates make good photo stops.

In wet weather: the path can be muddy in places. Wellies or sturdy shoes are sensible after rain. Generally well-drained.
