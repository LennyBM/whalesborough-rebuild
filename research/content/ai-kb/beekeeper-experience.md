# Beekeeper for a Day

**BLUF:** A four-hour immersive experience with the estate beekeeper — suit up, inspect the hives, watch frame extraction, take home a jar of estate honey. £125pp; adults only (16+); maximum four guests per session. Runs spring through autumn (typically April-October). Subject to weather and bee temperament.

The session: 09:30 arrival at the beekeeper's cabin, suit-up and safety briefing, walk to the 8-hive apiary on the meadow's edge, inspect 2-3 hives in detail (queen-spotting, drone-spotting, varroa-check), watch a frame uncapped and spin extraction, taste honey direct from the comb, return to the cabin for the takeaway jar and a hot drink.

The bees at Whalesborough are Cornish Black Bees — the native dark European honeybee, slowly being restored across the southwest after a century of imported Italian dominance. The colonies total around 15,000 bees across the apiary at peak season.

What to wear: long trousers, closed shoes, light-coloured top under the bee suit. Watch out for: known severe insect-sting allergy. People who carry adrenaline auto-injectors for sting reactions should not book — there is no medical override.

Weather and bee mood: bee colonies do not get inspected on cold, wet or stormy days (under 15°C, rain over light drizzle, winds 20mph+). The beekeeper's call is final; sessions postpone to a future date or refund in full.
