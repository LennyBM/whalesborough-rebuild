# Neetfield Market Garden tours

**BLUF:** Weekly free guided tours of Neetfield Market Garden — the organic vegetable plot that supplies The Weir Restaurant. Wednesdays at 14:00, 45 minutes. Led by the head gardener. Suitable for all ages. Book in the app.

The garden covers around 1 acre divided into raised beds, polytunnels and a wildflower border. Crops rotate through the seasons — leafy greens, brassicas, root crops, herbs, edible flowers, soft fruit, and the famous 22,000-pumpkin-seed autumn planting.

The tour walks the beds, explaining: the no-dig method (compost mulch layers), the on-site composting cycle, the pollinator strips that feed the bees, the seed-saving programme, what's harvested for the kitchen that morning. Tastings of what's in season — direct from the plant.

The gardener answers questions: how to start a no-dig bed at home, which crop is hardest, what fails. There is no merchandise to sell; this is a walking conversation with a working gardener.

For schools and bigger group bookings, the gardener takes pre-arranged sessions at school-rate weekday mornings during term-time.

In autumn (October), the pumpkin patch is open for "pick your own" — small pumpkins free with a stay, large ones £4-8.
