# Estate sustainability tours

**BLUF:** A monthly Friday-evening sustainability talk and tasting walks guests through the estate's energy, water and biodiversity systems — wind turbine, biomass boiler, microplastic filters, pollinator meadows. Free for in-stay guests; £15 for day visitors. Hosted by the head of sustainability or a guest specialist.

Format: 17:30 arrival at the spa cafe for a Cornish charcuterie and Camel Valley wine welcome; 18:00 a 45-minute walking tour visiting the turbine base, the biomass plant room (clean, well-lit), the microplastic filter installation in the laundry, the bee meadow and a brief glance at the rewilded woodland edge; 19:00 return to the cafe for tasting and conversation.

This is the talk that puts numbers behind the marketing claims. You leave knowing the kWh from the turbine, the actual microfibre count being captured, the number of bee colonies, the woodland area planted. The session also names what is not yet finished — the energy self-sufficiency target slipped its 2025 date and the current honest figure is shared.

Capacity 12. Booking in the app on the events tab. Comfortable shoes — the walk includes gravel paths. Indoor alternative if the weather is poor.

For deeper-dive private tours (small group, technical content), the head of sustainability takes pre-arranged groups by appointment.
