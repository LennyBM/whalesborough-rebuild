# Tintagel and Tintagel Castle

**BLUF:** Tintagel is about 30 minutes' drive south of Whalesborough on the dramatic north Cornish coast. The main draw is Tintagel Castle — English Heritage ruins on a clifftop island connected by a striking high footbridge, steeped in Arthurian legend. Allow at least 2-3 hours including the village.

What to see: the castle island (walk across the modern footbridge installed in 2019; spectacular views), the village of Tintagel (small, gift shops, the Old Post Office a 14th-century National Trust property, places to eat), and the cliff walks on the South West Coast Path.

Admission: English Heritage members enter the castle island free; non-members £15 adult, £9 child (current rates; confirm at English Heritage). The bridge and surrounding cliff areas are free to access.

Practicalities: park in the village car park (paid; £4-6 day). The walk down to the castle and bridge from the village is steep — a Land Rover shuttle is available for £5 return for less mobile guests. Wheelchair access onto the castle island via the footbridge is limited; check English Heritage current accessibility.

Allow time for Tintagel Old Post Office (£6 entry, NT members free) — a quirky historic building with one of the rare surviving 14th-century floor structures in Cornwall.

Worth combining with: a drive to Boscastle (5 miles south) for a coastal harbour wander, or to Crackington Haven for a coastal walk.
