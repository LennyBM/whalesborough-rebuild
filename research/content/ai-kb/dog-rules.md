# Dog rules at Whalesborough

**BLUF:** Dogs are welcome in 31 of the 34 units across the estate. The standard limit is two dogs per cottage (some cottages cap at one or two; see specific unit). Dogs must be kept on a lead in shared spaces, are not allowed inside the spa, the indoor pool or the restaurant's indoor seating, and must not be left unattended in cottages.

Dog-free units (no dogs accepted): Arvor Suite 1.0 (2LP), Penthouse 2.0 (4EL), and Eagle's Nest. Beachcombers caps at two dogs. Kew Villa and Nan's House cap at two dogs.

Inside cottages: dogs are welcome on the ground floor; bedrooms are typically off limits unless owner controls. Dog beds, throws and crates are provided in dog-friendly units. The standard expectation is that dogs do not sleep on beds and are not left alone for extended periods (under two hours is the practical guidance — longer becomes a welfare concern and risks barking that affects neighbours).

Across the estate dogs must be picked up after. Bag dispensers are at the main car parks and bin points. The estate's working farm has livestock — alpacas, goats, pigs, sheep, ducks — so dogs must remain on a lead near paddocks at all times.

The W Club spa is dog-free indoors but the outdoor terrace permits well-behaved dogs at the table. The Weir Restaurant welcomes dogs on the outdoor terrace and in the bar area; the main dining room is dog-free.
