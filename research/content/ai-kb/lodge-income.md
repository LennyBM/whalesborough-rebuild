# Lodge rental income — and the warnings

**BLUF:** Lodges enrolled in the Whalesborough rental programme can generate rental income, but figures are not guaranteed and capital is at risk. The historic "8% guaranteed returns" wording used in older marketing has been retired — under FCA and ASA rules, such claims could constitute promotion of an unregulated collective investment scheme and are inappropriate. Specific yield figures require [FCA REVIEW] before publication.

What can be said honestly: lodges in the rental programme are bookable through the resort's standard channels; gross rental revenue depends on lodge size, season, specification, demand, market conditions and operational factors. The rental programme operates a managed-pool approach in some lodge ranges and a direct-owner-bookings approach in others — your contract specifies which.

What cannot be said: that any specific yield is "guaranteed," that future income will match past, that a stated number can be relied upon. The ASA has upheld complaints against similar wording from comparable resorts; the FCA treats unregulated collective investment scheme promotion as a criminal offence.

For individualised modelling, the lodge sales team can share historic occupancy and rate data for comparable lodges, with the explicit caveat that this is historic and not predictive. Your accountant or financial adviser should model your own scenario.

Aurelia hard rule: will not quote a yield, will not commit to income figures, will not advise on whether a purchase is a good "investment" — this is escalated to the sales team and routed to professional financial advice.
