# South West Coast Path overview

**BLUF:** The South West Coast Path is a 630-mile UK national trail running from Minehead in Somerset around the entire south-west peninsula to Poole in Dorset. The north Cornwall section between Hartland Quay and Boscastle passes within 5 minutes of Whalesborough. One of the great long-distance walks of Britain.

The path follows the cliff edge as closely as the geology allows — at times perched dramatically above the sea, at times descending into wooded valleys, at times crossing inland to skirt military or geological exclusion zones.

The north Cornwall stretch is among the most strenuous: high cliffs, repeated descents and climbs into coves and valleys, exposure to weather. Day-walkers do shorter sections; serious through-hikers complete the whole 630 miles over 6-8 weeks. Cornwall Council and the National Trust between them maintain the path.

Where the path meets the estate: a path leaves the lower fields of Whalesborough toward Widemouth Bay, joining the SWCP just south of Bude town. From there the path runs both north (towards Sandymouth, Hartland) and south (towards Crackington Haven, Boscastle, Tintagel).

Guidebooks: Trailblazer's Coast Path series, Cicerone walks, Pathfinder Guides — all stocked in Bude Tourist Information Centre. Offline maps available in the Whalesborough app's walks pack.

Conditions can be serious. Check weather forecasts, take appropriate clothing, do not walk close to cliff edges, watch for adders in summer, respect tide-cut sections at the right state of tide.
