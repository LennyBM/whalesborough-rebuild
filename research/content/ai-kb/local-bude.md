# Bude — the town

**BLUF:** Bude is a laid-back north Cornwall coastal town about three miles north of Whalesborough. Officially the sunniest place in the UK in 2013. Architecture from the 16th century through to the Victorian period. Independent shops, surf cafes, dog-friendly pubs, the Bude Sea Pool at Summerleaze Beach, and the famous Bude Canal.

What to do in Bude: walk Summerleaze Beach (dogs October-mid-May), try Bude Sea Pool (free, year-round), browse the independent shops on Belle Vue and Lansdown Road, lunch at one of the many cafes, surf at Summerleaze or Crooklets, walk the canal towpath south back toward Whalesborough.

Where to eat in Bude (a short selection that gets repeat recommendations): The Bank Bistro and Bar, The Bay View Inn (dog-friendly), Mantra Bistro, Olive Garden, Life's a Beach (beachfront cafe), the Coastline Café, plenty of fish and chips at Summerleaze. The Bude Tourist Information Centre on the Crescent has the up-to-date local restaurant list.

Shopping: independents dominate. Cornish gifts, surf gear, books, art, jewellery, ceramics, locally made fudge. The Cornish Garden Pottery is a notable craft stop.

Travelling there: 5-10 minutes by car from the estate; 30 minutes walk via the canal towpath; 10 minutes by taxi.
