### 2.1 Background

A cookie is a small text file placed on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, pixels, tags, software development kits (SDKs) and device fingerprinting — we refer to all of these as "cookies" in this notice.

The Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 ("PECR"), as amended by the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 ("DUAA"), require us to obtain your consent before placing cookies on your device that are not strictly necessary. The law changed on 5 February 2026; fines for non-compliance can now reach £17.5 million or 4% of annual global turnover. We take cookie compliance seriously.

### 2.2 The new DUAA analytics exemption

Under DUAA 2025, certain analytics cookies are exempt from consent if:

- Their sole purpose is to gather aggregate statistical information about visitors to the website;
- Information is not shared with a third party for any other purpose;
- The third-party provider acts solely on our behalf and not for their own purposes.

We use **Plausible Analytics** in a self-hosted or first-party configuration that we believe qualifies for this exemption. Even so, we display information about these cookies and offer an easy opt-out as best practice. If at any time we change provider or configuration and the exemption no longer applies, we will move analytics to the "consent required" category and update this notice.

### 2.3 How we obtain your consent

Where consent is required (functional, analytics that does not qualify for the DUAA exemption, and marketing cookies), we obtain it via a cookie banner that:

- Displays "Accept all", "Reject all" and "Manage preferences" with equal prominence — no pre-ticked boxes, no greyed-out reject button, no cookie walls;
- Lets you set preferences at category level and refine to individual cookie level;
- Records your choices in a consent log with timestamp, IP (truncated), policy version, and the specific cookies enabled;
- Allows you to change your choices at any time via the persistent "Cookie settings" link in the footer.

Choosing "Reject all" places only strictly necessary cookies (and DUAA-exempt analytics if applicable). You can still book a stay, complete a transaction and use most of the platform without optional cookies. Some convenience features (e.g. remembering a saved itinerary) require functional cookies.

### 12.1 Our commitment

We want every guest to experience Whalesborough Farm Resort & Spa equally well, whether you visit our estate or our digital platform. This statement explains the accessibility level we are aiming for, what we have done, what we know is not yet there, and how to get help.

### 12.2 The standard we aim to meet

The Whalesborough platform is being designed and tested to **conform to Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) version 2.2, Level AA**. WCAG 2.2 is the standard published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) and is the recognised benchmark for the Equality Act 2010 and the European Accessibility Act as adopted in the UK.

In practice this means:

- **Keyboard operability:** every interactive element can be reached and operated using a keyboard alone, with a visible focus indicator of at least 2 px;
- **Target size:** every interactive control is at least 24 × 24 CSS pixels, with adequate spacing for finger and pointer use;
- **Contrast:** text colour and background combinations meet at least the 4.5 : 1 ratio for body text and 3 : 1 for large text and non-text user-interface elements;
- **Alt text:** every meaningful image carries descriptive alternative text; decorative images carry an empty alt attribute and a role marker so screen readers can skip them;
- **Date and time pickers:** every booking field can be completed without drag or mouse — fully typeable, keyboard-navigable, and screen-reader compatible;
- **Forms:** every input has a visible, persistent label; errors are described in plain English; error focus moves to the offending field;
- **Headings and landmarks:** every page uses a single H1, hierarchical headings, and ARIA landmarks (header, nav, main, footer) to allow rapid navigation;
- **Video:** videos have synchronised captions and an audio-described track or, where this is not technically practicable, a text transcript;
- **Documents:** PDF brochures are tagged for accessibility; where this is not yet the case an HTML alternative is provided;
- **Motion:** all decorative motion respects the user's `prefers-reduced-motion` setting.

### 12.3 What we have done

- Built the platform on the Coastal Editorial design system, which embeds accessibility tokens, focus rings and target sizes by default;
- Used semantic HTML5, ARIA only where semantic HTML cannot do the job, and live-region announcements for booking-step updates;
- Trained our design and development team in WCAG 2.2 Level AA;
- Run automated checks (axe-core) on every build, plus manual screen reader testing with NVDA, VoiceOver and TalkBack;
- Conducted a third-party accessibility audit — [AUDITOR NAME, AUDIT DATE]; **[ACCESSIBILITY AUDITOR REQUIRED — to be commissioned before launch; insert auditor name and report reference here]**.
