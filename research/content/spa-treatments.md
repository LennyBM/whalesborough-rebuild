## How the menu is organised

The W Club menu sits across four lines. Each one answers a different need — and each is anchored in the same Gaia apothecary.

| Line | What it does | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Gaia Rituals** | The signature line. Multi-stage journeys built around three oil blends — Calming, Awakening, Balancing — plus longer ceremonial work. | Guests who want a full experience, not a single technique. |
| **Facials** | Considered skin work. Plant actives, jade wands, lifting massage, lymphatic drainage. | Skin that wants attention without surgery, needles or downtime. |
| **Body & Massage** | Honest, technical bodywork. Salt, hot stone, Indian head, bespoke pressure. | Tension, travel ache, training fatigue, a long week. |
| **Hands & Feet** | Quiet, polished, properly done. | A short window, a wedding morning, a Friday afternoon. |

All rituals open with a brief consultation, a foot wash with Cornish sea salt, and three breaths under a warm towel. That part is not on the menu. It happens anyway.

### The Gaia Calming Journey — 60 min

**BLUF.** A back, neck and shoulder massage layered with scalp work and a hand-and-arm sequence, using the Gaia Calming oil blend. Sixty minutes engineered for the run-down — quiet pressure, long strokes, lavender and chamomile.

The ritual opens in the consultation room with the Calming blend warmed in your palms — lavender drawn from the estate's walled market garden, Roman chamomile, a thread of vetiver to ground the formulation. You are draped face-down on a heated table. The therapist works first the back, slowly, with the heel of the hand and the forearm; then the neck, where most of the week is held; then the shoulders, then the scalp. The hand-and-arm sequence comes last, palms-up, slow, almost ceremonial.

> *Lavender from the market garden, warm hands, sixty minutes that don't ask anything of you.*

What you feel is not euphoria. It is the slow withdrawal of held tension — the jaw releasing first, then the shoulders, then somewhere behind the eyes. Most guests describe the back of the neck as the place that finally lets go. Sleep is often deeper the night after. The Calming oil leaves a soft, dry-down scent that lingers into the evening.

This is the gentlest entry into the Gaia line. Suitable for first-time spa guests, for men who haven't had a massage in a year, for anyone presenting with the particular fatigue that comes from too many screens and not enough Cornwall.
