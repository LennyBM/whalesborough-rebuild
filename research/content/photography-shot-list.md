*Compiled 14 May 2026 · Peake Management for Whalesborough Farm Resort & Spa · Companion to `asset-creation-brief.md` Section 2 · Coastal Editorial design system*

---

## 0. How to Read This Brief

Every shot below is a self-contained production card. The photographer, producer and stylist should be able to walk on set with this document and execute without re-briefing the agency. Each card answers ten questions: what, where, when, how, with what camera, in what style, with what crops, with what release, with what props, and where it ends up.

Editorial reference frame for the whole commission:
- **Cereal magazine, Volume 18** — for the slow-light interior philosophy and the absence of clutter. Pages 34–47 (Helsinki interior feature) for the warm-white tonality.
- **Heckfield Place 2024 brochure** — for the "lived-in luxury" cottage interiors with the wood-burner lit, the bread on the counter, the dog on the rug.
- **The Newt Hotel collateral** — for the farm and garden environmental portraits (beekeeper, gardener) where the human is small in the frame and the landscape does the talking.
- **Aman Resorts on-property photography** — for the unpeopled spa work, the negative space, the colour grading toward warm parchment rather than cool teal.
- **Toa Heftiba (Heckfield) and Joe Fletcher (Aman)** — additional behavioural references for the photographer to study before shoot day.

Common-to-all-shots specifications (do not repeat per card):
- Camera body: Sony A7R V (61MP) or Phase One IQ3 100MP for hero plates. Backup: Sony A7 IV.
- Format delivery: RAW + 16-bit TIFF + sRGB JPG at 100% + AVIF + WebP, plus all named crops.
- Colour space on camera: AdobeRGB. Delivery to web: sRGB. Print: AdobeRGB.
- White balance: shot in daylight 5500K unless noted; graded warm in post (slight magenta-yellow lift on highlights, sage-cyan shadow lift).
- Sharpening: minimal. No clarity slider at all. Texture +5 max.
- Skin tones: warm but never orange. Cornish daylight reference; not Mediterranean.
- Lens guidance: 24mm GM, 35mm GM, 50mm GM, 85mm GM, 24-70mm GM II zoom for run-and-gun, 100mm macro for food and detail.
- Tripod: Gitzo Mountaineer 3-series with RRS BH-55 head. Required on all interior and dawn/dusk shots.
- Lighting: natural-light first. Where supplemental needed, use Aputure 600x / 300x bounced through diffusion, never on-camera flash, never bare-bulb. Practicals (table lamps, fire) always on for ambient warmth.
- Post-production house: London or Bristol editorial retoucher (see Suppliers in main brief). Budget £8–12k against the photography line.

### WB-001 — Drone aerial estate, dawn

- **Category:** Hero / Aerial
- **Subject:** Whole-estate establishing shot from 120m AGL, looking south-east toward the Tamar valley, with mist hanging in the field hollows and the farmhouse + cottage cluster catching first light.
- **Location on estate:** Drone takeoff from the upper paddock north of Whalesborough Farmhouse. Permitted flight corridor pre-cleared with CAA + landowner (already in place).
- **Time of day:** Civil twilight starting 15 minutes before sunrise. Flight window 04:45–05:30 in October, 05:30–06:30 in spring.
- **Season window:** Late October 2026 (mist most likely after a still, cold night following a damp afternoon) and a backup attempt April 2027.
- **Camera spec:** DJI Inspire 3 with Zenmuse X9-8K Air. Full-frame 8K stills extracted from ProRes RAW capture. ND8 + ND16 filters on hand. Tripod N/A (drone).
- **Style notes:** Low-velocity tracking move (5 km/h max) to give the stills crew clean frames at multiple compositions. Highlights protected; expose for the mist's brightness. No post-flash; this is a pure natural-light dawn frame. Focal interest: the chimneys of the farmhouse rising from the mist.
- **Composition:** Rule-of-thirds with the farmhouse on the lower-right intersection, mist in lower-third, sky two-thirds, distant coast as a thin band on the horizon line. Generous negative space top-left for headline overlay.
- **Crops needed:** 21:9 ultra-wide (3840×1646), 16:9 hero (1920×1080), 4:5 portrait social (1080×1350), 1:1 social (1080×1080), 1200×630 OG.
- **Talent / model release:** Not required.
- **Props / styling:** Pre-arrange estate so visible cottages have lights on (timer-controlled). Ensure no parked vehicles in frame — staff to clear forecourt by 04:00.
- **Reference image:** Heckfield Place 2024 brochure aerial spread, page 4–5. Cereal vol. 18 mist-photography reference.
- **Where it lives:** Homepage hero (primary). Estate Navigator landing page. Brand film opening frame. Investor deck cover.
- **Priority:** P1
- **Estimated time on shoot day:** 1.5 hours including setup, flight, take-down. Two flight slots if first batch is fogged out.
- **Estimated post-production cost:** Medium (mist plate enhancement, sky retouch if grey, two-frame composite for clouds if necessary).

### 9.9 Editorial style consistency notes

The whole commission references **Cereal magazine vol. 18** specifically — the slow-light interior philosophy, the deliberate absence of clutter, the warm-white tonality on pages 34–47 (Helsinki interior feature) and the food spreads on pages 60–63. Beyond that, the photographer should study:
- **Heckfield Place 2024 brochure** for cottage interiors with the wood-burner lit, bread on the counter, dog on the rug — the "lived-in luxury" register.
- **The Newt Hotel collateral** for farm and garden environmental portraits where the human is small in the frame and the landscape does the talking.
- **Aman Resorts on-property photography** for the unpeopled spa work, the negative space, the colour grading toward warm parchment rather than cool teal.
- **Soneva Soul and Soneva Fushi marketing collateral** for the spa-blue-hour register and product flat-lays.

Avoid:
- Real-estate-style HDR wide-angle distortion.
- Over-saturated Instagram filtration.
- Stock-photo "Mediterranean orange" skin tones.
- Lens flares used as ornament.
- Anything that reads as "AI-generated luxury."
