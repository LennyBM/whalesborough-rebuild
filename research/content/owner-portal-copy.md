## 3. `/owners/property/[id]/rental` — Rental Income Page

**Headline**
*Rental performance — {plot_ref}.*

**Sub**
What the lodge has earned, month by month, what came off the top, what reached you. Actuals only.

### Intro (500 words)

This page shows what your lodge has actually earned since the rental scheme activated. Every figure is a recorded transaction, every deduction is itemised against an invoice, every payout matches what landed in your bank account. There are no projections on this page. There are no models, no extrapolations, no "expected" columns. If a number is on this screen, it has happened.

We have built it this way for a reason. When you committed to a Whalesborough lodge you read marketing material that included a rental return narrative; that narrative described how the scheme is structured and what historical occupancy on this part of the Cornish coast has looked like. Marketing material does what marketing material has to do — it sells a possibility. The portal does the opposite job. It shows the recorded reality, transparently, without rounding, without the gentler verbs. We think you would rather have it that way, and we think you bought in for the long-game version of this anyway.

The headline tile at the top shows year-to-date net rental income — gross paid by guests, minus VAT where applicable, minus the management commission stated in your rental agreement, minus housekeeping costs charged at trade rate, minus consumables and ad-hoc spend on the lodge, minus any pre-agreed reserve. The figure under it is the same number twelve months ago, on the same date, on this lodge or — for lodges in their first year — on the comparable benchmark used in your underwriting model. We label that benchmark explicitly. You will never see a peer-park average dressed up as your own performance.

The monthly chart underneath plots two lines. The thicker line is your gross before deductions. The thinner line below it is your net after everything. The space between them is what running the lodge cost — visible at a glance, never hidden, totalled in the statements drawer. The chart goes back to month one of the rental scheme and forward only as far as actual departures; the future is not drawn until the bookings have happened and the guests have left.

Below the chart, the deductions panel shows every line that came off. Management commission. Housekeeping per turnaround. Consumables (sundries, welcome hamper, replenishments). VAT recovered or paid. Maintenance jobs categorised against the priority levels in your service agreement. Marketing levy if applicable. Each row links to the underlying invoice; tap any row to read the invoice itself, see who signed it off, and (if it is something you would not have approved) raise a query that reaches {concierge_name} within ten minutes.

The projected panel at the foot of the page is the only part of this screen that looks forward, and we treat it with the care it deserves. It shows confirmed bookings already taken, departing in the next ninety days, at the rate they were sold at. It is not a forecast of unsold nights, and it does not estimate the income of months further out. We label it *confirmed forward income*, not *projected income*. The language matters. Past performance does not guarantee future returns. Your income may be lower or higher than recent months and depends on occupancy, market conditions, and individual circumstances. ## 4. `/owners/property/[id]/occupancy` — Occupancy Calendar

**Headline**
*Calendar — {plot_ref}.*

**Sub**
Twelve months ahead, colour-coded. Tap any week to act on it.

### Intro (400 words)

The calendar shows every night on your lodge for the year ahead, colour-coded by status. **Green** is a confirmed rental booking — guest name and headcount visible to you only, never publicly. **Cream** is held for owner personal use, blocked by you. **Soft grey** is held for maintenance or commissioning, blocked by us with a reason note on each block. **Open white** is available and earning — the rental engine is taking enquiries against it. **Pale gold** is a soft hold — an enquiry currently in basket, not yet confirmed; releases automatically within four hours if not converted.

You can block personal-use weeks straight from this view. Tap a week, choose *Block for owner use*, confirm. If the week is currently available it is held within the hour and the rental engine stops offering it. If the week is already sold to a guest, you cannot override their booking — we honour every confirmed reservation. If the week is one of the four school-holiday peak weeks (Easter fortnight, late July to August, October half-term, Christmas), the system routes the request into the annual ballot rather than confirming immediately; see the rules below.

The cap is six personal-use weeks per calendar year. The portal counts down as you block, shows the running tally in the header, and refuses a seventh block with a tooltip explaining the rule. If circumstances change — a wedding, a bereavement, a sudden need for somewhere quiet — the concierge line can release an extra week per year on compassionate grounds, no fee, no questions, one tap on the *Speak to {concierge_name}* button.

**Peak-week allocation.** The four school-holiday peaks (Easter, summer, October half-term, Christmas-New Year) are balloted in November for the following year because demand from both rental guests and owner households outstrips supply. Every owner gets one guaranteed peak week of their choosing if they enter the ballot by 30 November; second peak weeks are allocated by lottery, with priority weighted toward owners who took fewer peaks the previous year. Owners who do not enter the ballot lose only that year's peak allocation — your six-week annual cap is unaffected.

**Lead time on non-peak weeks.** Fourteen days minimum, three months maximum forward booking for personal use. The fourteen-day cut-off protects guests already searching; the three-month ceiling stops the calendar locking up against the rental engine. Same-day, same-week, and "we are in the area, can we drop in" requests are usually possible through {concierge_name} — most are granted; the portal just cannot guarantee them.

## 7. `/owners/property/[id]/personal-use` — Block Personal Weeks

**Headline**
*Your weeks here.*

**Sub**
Six per calendar year. The rules below are deliberate. Read them once.

### Intro (400 words)

Six weeks. The cap was set when the rental scheme was designed and it is the same for every lodge owner across Trelowen, Tevi, Gwelva and Bespoke. The reason is not arbitrary. Below six weeks the rental scheme economics work — there is enough sellable inventory through peak, shoulder, and trough seasons to underwrite the income narrative. Above six weeks the model breaks; we cannot maintain rental performance and we cannot sustain the maintenance cycle that keeps the lodge to a five-star-gold finish. The cap is a load-bearing wall, not a wishlist item. If you need more time on-site we can usually find it — a free midweek inside another owner's blocked week, a cancellation, a soft hold against a sales viewing — but the structural number is six.

**How the cap counts.** A week runs Friday to Friday, the rental industry standard, seven nights. Partial weeks count proportionally — a long weekend of three nights is 0.43 weeks, deducted from your six. You can mix and match — five full weeks plus seven scattered weekends totals six. The portal tracks the running figure and refuses any block that would exceed it.

**Lead time.** Fourteen days minimum, three months maximum forward booking. Inside fourteen days the lodge may already be selling to guests and we cannot pull confirmed bookings. Beyond three months the calendar must remain open to fair rental pricing for everyone. Same-day and short-notice requests are usually possible through {concierge_name} — most are granted.

**Peak-week ballot.** Easter fortnight, late July through August, October half-term, the week between Christmas and New Year. Demand for these weeks from rental guests is multiple times supply, and demand from owner households is multiple times supply again. The ballot runs in November for the following year. Every owner who enters gets one guaranteed peak week of their first-choice preference, subject to clashing first-choices being separated by random draw. Second peaks are allocated by lottery weighted toward owners who took fewer peaks the previous year. Miss the November ballot deadline and you forfeit that year's peak allocation — your six-week annual cap is unaffected, but your weeks must fall in non-peak periods.

**Compassionate release.** Bereavement, parental leave, serious illness, a wedding that has to land on a date the ballot did not give you. One call to {concierge_name}. We have never refused a compassionate request in twelve years.

## 9. `/owners/community` — Private Owner Social Feed

**Headline**
*Owners' feed.*

**Sub**
Forty-odd households on the estate. The kettle on, more or less.

### Intro (250 words)

This is the private feed for Whalesborough lodge owners. No staff, no guests, no public visibility — only the people who own a lodge here, and the small number of Whalesborough team members tagged with explicit owner-portal permissions (Rebecca, your concierge, the estate director). Anything posted here stays inside this community.

People use it for the obvious things. Restaurant recommendations on the coast — which Bude bistro is doing the spider crab properly this month, which St Mawes pub kept the dogs out. Trade recommendations — the gardener who came to lift their hedge, the housekeeper they trust to drop in mid-week. Recipes — the kitchen-garden glut produces tomatoes nobody knows what to do with, and the feed sorts that out collectively. Bookings exchanged — "I am holding the second week of July, but plans changed, anyone want it before I release back to the rental engine" — which is one of the kinder uses of the feed. Notes about wildlife sightings, the kingfisher on the lake, the new alpaca cria.

There is no algorithm. Newest at the top, chronological, mute any thread you do not want to follow. Reactions only, no quote-tweeting. Whalesborough does not mine or monetise the feed — we do not even read it unless tagged in. The point is that you own here, and there are other people who own here, and we noticed nobody had built them a way to talk to one another.

### Community guidelines (200 words)

**Be kind.** This is a small community — forty-odd households, the same names appearing each week. Read your post twice before you press send.

**Keep it about the estate, the coast, or owning here.** Politics, religion, market timing, capital growth speculation, and the rental-yield comparisons that always start with "I'm just curious what you're seeing on yours" are outside the lines of this feed. Founder Circle owner-only events are the right place for those conversations face to face.

**No selling.** The community is not a marketplace. Selling your lodge runs through the resale page. Trade recommendations are welcome; trade promotion is not. If you are an owner who also runs a business that other owners might use, mention it once when relevant — do not seed posts.

**Privacy.** Anything posted here is private to owners but is not legally confidential. Assume a screenshot could leave the room. Do not post photographs of children other than your own. Do not name guests who have stayed in your lodge under the rental scheme.

**Moderation.** Light-touch. {concierge_name} will only remove a post if it breaks the guidelines or names a non-consenting party. Anyone unhappy with moderation can speak directly to the estate director — open door, always.

### Lead (600 words)

The lodge takes between sixteen and twenty-two weeks from foundations to handover, depending on whether you have chosen a standard or a customised specification, the time of year, and the weather. **Phase two — shell.** Three to five weeks. **Phase three — first fix.** Three to four weeks. Electrical wiring run through walls. Plumbing first-fix to all wet rooms. **Phase four — fit-out.** Three to five weeks. Plastering and skimming. Kitchen install (Symphony or Howdens, finish to your customisation order). Wardrobes, soft furnishings, the furniture pack you chose at reservation. Lighting commissioned. Hot tub installed if your plot specification includes it. By the end of phase four the lodge is essentially finished.

We hand over a lodge with our snagging list closed; the owner snagging window (covered separately) runs for twenty-eight days after you have the keys.

## 16. Exit / Programme Transfer FAQs

**Headline**
*Ending the rental scheme, transferring ownership, end-of-licence questions.*

**Sub**
Ten of the questions owners most often ask. The full programme terms are in your document vault under *Rental Scheme Agreement v3* and *125-Year Licence Pack*.

---

**1. Can I withdraw from the rental scheme without selling the lodge?**

Yes. The rental scheme is opt-in and you can opt out with six months' written notice via the portal or through {concierge_name}. The six-month window lets us close out forward bookings already taken and prevents guest disappointment. After the window closes, the rental engine stops offering your lodge, statements stop, and you take back full use of all 365 days. Your Founder Circle benefits, document vault, maintenance log and concierge line all remain — opting out of the rental scheme is not opting out of ownership.

---

**2. If I withdraw from the rental scheme, can I rejoin later?**

Yes, with three months' notice in the opposite direction. We need three months to recommission the lodge to scheme standard (refresh photographs, recompile the listing copy, run a deep clean, replace any soft furnishings that have aged out of scheme spec) before reopening to guests. There is no rejoining fee and no penalty.

---

**3. What happens if I want to sell the lodge but it is mid-rental-scheme with future bookings?**

Forward bookings already taken transfer to the new owner along with the lodge — they inherit both the income from those nights and the obligation to honour them. If the new owner does not wish to continue the rental scheme, the same six-month notice rule applies from the date of transfer. We always disclose forward bookings to the new owner at offer stage so there are no surprises.

---

**4. Can I transfer ownership to a family member without selling?**

Yes. Gift-transfers, transfers into a discretionary trust, transfers between joint owners on a divorce or death, transfers into a corporate structure — all supported. Each requires solicitor input on both sides because the 125-year licence has to reassign. Whalesborough does not charge a transfer fee for family or estate-planning transfers (we charge a £750 administrative fee for transfers into corporate structures to cover the AML and beneficial-ownership work involved).

---

**5. What happens at the end of the 125-year licence?**

The licence is the longest in the sector and runs to the year 2151 from the foundation date of the Trelowen, Tevi, Gwelva, and Bespoke developments. The estate retains a residual freehold interest; at licence end, the lodge and its plot revert to the estate. This is the standard structure for residential leases and lodge licences in the UK. Owners who want a freehold-equivalent permanent build can speak to us about the Bespoke and Gwelva architectural tiers, which sit on a different licence structure with no end-of-term reversion.

---

**6. What if I die during ownership — what happens to the lodge?**

The lodge passes through your estate exactly as any second property would. The 125-year licence and the rental scheme contract both survive death — your executors continue receiving rental income and statements; your beneficiaries inherit the lodge subject to the same licence terms. We work directly with executors during the probate window to keep operations running, pause the rental scheme on request if the family needs the lodge available, and reassign the documentation once probate clears. Joint ownership simplifies this entirely; sole ownership routes through the standard probate process.

---

**7. Can I rent the lodge privately, outside the Whalesborough rental scheme?**

No. The rental scheme is exclusive when you are enrolled, and the licence terms permit private rental only with prior written consent (which we have granted in three specific historical cases involving family use that crossed a commercial threshold). The rationale is operational — running a single, coordinated rental and maintenance scheme is what keeps the estate to five-star-gold standard for every guest. If you want full private control, opt out of the rental scheme on six months' notice.

---

**8. What happens if the Whalesborough rental scheme stops operating in future?**

The 125-year licence does not depend on the rental scheme continuing. If the scheme were ever to wind down (it has not in twelve years and there is no current plan for it to), owners would revert to full personal control of their lodges, the document vault and concierge structure would remain, and any final rental statements would be reconciled and paid. The licence itself is unaffected by any change to the rental product.

---

**9. Are there any restrictions on who I can sell my lodge to?**

The new owner must complete the same AML, identity, and source-of-funds checks every original owner went through. Whalesborough retains a right of approval — exercised only when the proposed buyer fails AML, has been a banned guest at the estate for cause, or is buying through a structure designed to evade scheme rules. We have used the approval right twice in twelve years; both times the buyer reapplied through a different structure and was approved.

---

**10. If circumstances force an urgent sale, how fast can the resale process move?**

Fourteen to twenty-six weeks is typical for a resale from listing to completion — driven mostly by the speed of buyer conveyancing rather than our side. For genuinely urgent cases (bereavement requiring estate distribution, medical relocation, financial hardship), the Whalesborough team will accelerate every controllable step: same-day photographs, week-one listing, priority routing through the qualified buyer database. We have closed a resale in nine weeks for a bereavement; that is the fastest in our history. If you anticipate an urgent sale, speak to {concierge_name} before listing — the team can quietly pre-position a small number of pre-qualified buyers.
