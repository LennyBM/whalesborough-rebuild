### Section: Voucher types explainer

**Eyebrow label**
THE FINE PRINT, MADE PLAIN

**Headline (h2)**
*What you can — and can't — do with a voucher.*

A monetary voucher can be used against any single purchase or split across several. It can be combined with another voucher (up to three per booking). It can pay the full balance or part of it. Unused balance stays on the code for the rest of the 24 months.

An experience voucher is a single redemption. The scope is fixed at the experience — a Spa Day for Two is exactly that, with the inclusions named on the voucher. If the recipient wants to add an extra course, an extra night, or an extra treatment, they pay the difference at the time.

Both types are non-refundable after the 14-day cooling-off window, non-transferable once redeemed, and not redeemable for cash. Both extend up to 24 months from issue date. Lost vouchers can be reissued from the original purchase record — keep your order confirmation email.
