# Benchmark: UK Holiday Lodge Sales Platforms (May 2026)

**Purpose:** Inform the Whalesborough Farm Resort & Spa £500k booking-app build, specifically the lodge-sales module. Covers competitor digital sales journeys, FCA/ASA-compliant copy patterns, UX recommendations, and the legal frame around 125-year licences, BS 3632, AML, and rental-income claims.

**Companion docs:** `uk-compliance-security.md` (sections 5 + 11) — the lodge-sales compliance baseline this benchmark builds on.

## 2. Best UX Patterns Observed

1. **Clear deposit ladder shown on the buying page (Gull Rocks).** £5,000 refundable within 14 days → 75% within one month → balance at handover. Removes the biggest source of buyer anxiety. URL: gullrocksbeach.co.uk/buying.
2. **Itemised site-fee breakdown (Gull Rocks: ~£6,868.68/yr).** Lists exactly what's covered (pitch, water/sewer, maintenance, security, waste, landscaping, Wi-Fi, seasonal facility access, annual golf ticket). Insurance and utilities itemised separately. This is the single most converting trust signal — competitors who hide it lose to those who show it.
3. **Owner-Hub portal as a marketing asset before purchase (Park Holidays).** Showing prospective buyers what they'll get post-purchase (account, billing, news, FAQs, owner events, referral form) materially de-risks the buy decision.
4. **Mobile-app interactive map (Away Resorts, by Intelligent Mobile).** A bespoke park map that lets owners route to their lodge / facility from current location. Same map can be re-skinned for the **plot-availability** map at the sales stage.
5. **Brochure-capture form that segments buyers (Luxury Lodges).** Budget band, timeline, preferred resort, interests. Routes the lead into the right sales-rep workflow rather than a generic enquiry.
6. **Owner-testimonial proof (Gull Rocks).** "Good return on our investment to cover the associated costs and to provide an income for our retirement." Owner-said-it framing is **not** an operator claim, which keeps it ASA-compliant.
7. **125-year (or 950-year at Bude Residences) tenure made visible.** Owners want certainty of duration; surfacing tenure on the lodge product page is conversion-positive.
8. **Tax-position bullets (Bude Residences).** "No stamp duty or council tax" with asterisked qualifier. The asterisk is what keeps it compliant.

## 3. Worst Pitfalls Observed (To Avoid)

1. **"Guaranteed rental returns of up to £X" — Bude Coastal Resort and Luxury Lodges Residences.** This is exactly the construction ASA upheld four complaints against in the Luxury Lodge Estates ruling (case A24-1256125). The combination of "guaranteed" + "up to" + a headline £ figure misleads consumers about whether the return is contractual, what occupancy was assumed, and what fees are deducted. **Whalesborough must not use this phrasing.**
2. **No risk disclosure.** None of the premium-tier operators surveyed (Gull Rocks excepted) carry "capital at risk", "past performance is not a guide to future performance", or a price-may-fall warning. Only **HPB**, the FCA-authorised one, does — and that's the model to copy.
3. **Hidden fees in headline price.** ASA cited Luxury Lodge Estates for omitting non-optional service charges from the "from £295k" headline. Whalesborough must show site-fee, utilities, insurance and ground-rent figures on the same page as the asking price.
4. **No virtual tour / no floor plan on premium-tier listings.** Gull Rocks justlodges listings show **19+ photos and a YouTube video but no Matterport and no floor plan**. At £450k a unit this is below 2026 expectations.
5. **No interactive plot map on any premium UK lodge operator surveyed.** Despite In-Site™ park-software supporting clickable availability/reserved/sold states, none of the surveyed operators expose it to the public. **Whalesborough has a clear differentiator here.**
6. **No comparison tool between lodge types.** Every operator presents lodges as a list. A side-by-side comparator (sleeps, beds, decking m², hot tub Y/N, price, site-fee tier, available plots) is absent across the market.
7. **Brochure as PDF download with no progressive disclosure.** A 24-page PDF behind a single email field is 2018-era. The market has moved to multi-step lead capture (budget, timeline, location, finance interest) — Luxury Lodges and Whalesborough's own existing `15-brochure-request` design already do this.
8. **Sales journey collapses at the viewing booking.** Every operator surveyed funnels to a phone number or generic contact form for viewings. A real calendar-pick UI is absent industry-wide — another differentiator.
9. **Soft AML/source-of-funds touchpoints.** No operator surveyed prepares buyers for the AML documentation a solicitor will require. Adding a short "what to expect at completion" explainer (ID, proof of funds, source of funds — Money Laundering Regulations 2017 / 2026 amendments) builds trust and shortens the deal.

## 4. Recommended Sales Journey UX — Whalesborough (Page by Page)

This maps to the existing `02-lodge-sales` through `18-contact` living-pages.

1. **Landing / hero (`02-lodge-sales`).** Hero image, signature gold-accent phrase, single-paragraph value prop. No yield numbers. CTAs: "Browse residences" + "Request brochure" + "Book a viewing".
2. **Product tier overview (`03-tevi-luxury`, `04-luxury-villas`).** Three-up cards (cottage / lodge / villa) with from-price, key specs, hero image. **From-price** allowed only if the lowest-price unit is genuinely available.
3. **Product detail page (per residence type).** Hero, photo gallery (12+ images, AVIF/WebP), **Matterport virtual tour** (industry gap — own it), **interactive floor plan** (zoomable SVG with hover-room captions), spec sheet (sleeps, beds, baths, decking m², BS 3632 confirmed, year-round-use note), site-fee for this product, included perks. Below the fold: similar residences carousel.
4. **Interactive plot map (`02-lodge-sales` subroute).** Top-down site plan, plots colour-coded **Available / Reserved / Sold / Owner-occupied**. Click plot → modal showing residence on that plot, view, orientation, "Reserve a viewing on this plot" CTA. (In-Site™ or custom SVG against a Postgres `plots` table.)
5. **Buyers' guide (`05-buyers-guide`).** Plain-English walk-through of: 125-year licence vs freehold, BS 3632:2023, site-fee mechanics, 11/12-month occupancy planning rules, the sublet option, what AML at completion looks like, what the solicitor does. **This page is the FCA-safety belt** — it explains, doesn't promise.
6. **The process (`06-the-process`).** 5-step ladder: Enquire → Viewing → Reserve (£5k refundable 14 days) → 75% within 30 days → Completion + handover. Mirror Gull Rocks' clear ladder.
7. **The costs (`07-the-costs`).** Itemised: asking price, deposit, site fees, utilities (metered, estimated band), insurance band, optional sublet management fee, optional extras (decking add-ons, hot tub). No "from" — actual ranges.
8. **Rental income page (`08-guaranteed-income` → rename `08-rental-income-potential`).** **Critical rename.** No "guaranteed". Page describes the sublet service, historic occupancy bands by season for the resort *as a whole* (not per-unit promises), management fee structure, what owners receive vs what is retained for cleaning/marketing. Mandatory three-line risk panel: capital at risk, past performance not a guide, occupancy varies — see Section 5 below.
9. **Make-some-money (`09-make-some-money`).** Optional. If kept, retitle "Earning potential" and frame entirely as worked example with "your results will vary" qualifier on every figure.
10. **Considerations (`10-what-to-consider`).** Mortgageability (most lenders don't lend on lodges — declare openly), insurance, capital depreciation reality, the 125-yr lease ticking down at resale.
11. **Perfect time (`11-perfect-time`).** Soft seasonal CTA — book viewing in spring/summer.
12. **FAQs (`12-faqs`).** 30+ Q&As — see `ai-llm-mega-page` skill pattern; doubles as AI-citation surface.
13. **Tours (`13-tours`).** **Real calendar-pick** with rep availability, location-of-viewing, party-size, mobility-needs. Industry gap.
14. **Waiting list (`14-waiting-list`).** For sold-out residences. Triggers email when matching plot frees.
15. **Brochure request (`15-brochure-request`).** Multi-step: budget band → timeline → resort/residence preference → finance interest → details → consent. Routes the lead.
16. **Cottage sales (`16-cottage-sales`).** Mirror structure for the smaller product tier.
17. **Brochure download (`17-brochure-download`).** Thank-you page; PDF link + first sales-rep callback time stated.
18. **Contact (`18-contact`).** Sales team, viewing line, switchboard, registered office, company number, ICO number.

**Post-purchase owner portal** — separate auth surface; see Section 7.

## 5. Compliant Copy Patterns (How to Talk About Income Without Breaching FCA/ASA)

The Luxury Lodge Estates ruling (ASA case A24-1256125, July 2025) makes the rules concrete. The CMS Law and FCA statements on unregulated holiday-park investment schemes reinforce them.

**Banned phrases:**
- "Guaranteed return / guaranteed rental income" (unless contractually guaranteed by a regulated party)
- "Up to £X" without disclosing assumed occupancy and fees
- "Investment opportunity" used alongside numeric return figures
- "Risk-free", "guaranteed yield", "guaranteed income for life"
- "X% return" without a corresponding fee, occupancy and risk disclosure

**Safe phrases (modelled on Gull Rocks, HPB and Park Holidays):**
- "A lifestyle purchase with the option to let when not in use"
- "Owners who choose to sublet through our managed programme have the potential to earn rental income; figures vary by season, occupancy and unit"
- "Our 2024 sublet programme delivered an average gross rental income of £X across all participating residences" (historic, average, all-units — **not** "you will earn")
- "Past performance is not a guide to future performance"
- "Your residence is a depreciating asset; capital values can fall as well as rise"
- "Site fees and utility costs are payable annually whether the residence is let or not"
- "The lease has a fixed term of 125 years from purchase and reduces with each onward sale"
- "Whalesborough Farm Resort Sales Limited is not authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority. This product is not a regulated investment. You will not be entitled to compensation from the Financial Services Compensation Scheme or the Financial Ombudsman Service if things go wrong."

**Mandatory risk panel (every lodge-sales page that mentions income):**
> *Owning a residence at Whalesborough is a lifestyle decision. If you choose to sublet, rental income will vary with occupancy, season and market conditions and is not guaranteed. The residence is a depreciating asset and its resale value may be lower than the price paid. Site fees, utilities and insurance are payable regardless of letting. This is not a regulated investment.*

**Where it goes:** sticky panel at the foot of pages 02, 03, 04, 08, 09, 15.

## 6. Interactive Plot Map References

**The gap:** no surveyed UK premium lodge operator publishes a live availability-aware plot map on their public site. **In-Site™ park-management software** has this as a backend feature (filter by available, outstanding balance, age, make), but operators keep it internal.

**Closest public-facing references (re-skin patterns, not full lodge-sales examples):**
- **Holiday Park Maps Ltd** (holidayparkmaps.com) — supplies 700+ UK parks with custom illustrated SVGs; we can commission a Whalesborough variant.
- **In-Site™ park map module** (insite-parks.co.uk/Features/ParkMap) — backend filter pattern to replicate.
- **Beverley Holidays park maps** — clickable illustrated map with facility pop-ups (information-only, no state).
- **Sherwood Forest Holiday Park** — pitch-level navigable map (same pattern, no state).
- **Away Resorts mobile app** — bespoke interactive map by Intelligent Mobile.

**Recommended Whalesborough implementation:**
- Top-down illustrated SVG of the estate
- Plot polygons with `data-state` of `available | reserved | sold | owner-occupied` from Postgres
- Hover → tooltip (residence name, sleeps, from-price)
- Click → side-drawer with full residence detail + "Book a viewing on this plot" CTA
- WCAG 2.2 AA — keyboard-navigable plots, ARIA labels, list-fallback for screen readers
- No outbound JS to third parties (CSP discipline)

## 8. Calculator and Comparison Tool Recommendations

**Industry gap:** no UK lodge operator surveyed exposes a public ROI calculator (the only ones in the Google results are generic landlord-yield calculators by Foxtons, Savills, Landlord Vision). After the ASA ruling, putting a yield calculator on the public sales site is **high-risk**.

**Recommended approach:**
- **No public ROI calculator on the marketing site.** Industry-best after ASA Luxury Lodge Estates.
- **Total Cost of Ownership tool**, public — sums purchase price + site fee + utilities + insurance + optional sublet mgmt fee over a chosen hold period. Outputs cost-of-ownership, not yield. Compliant.
- **Comparison tool**, public — side-by-side three residences (sleeps, beds, baths, decking, hot tub, pets, from-price, site-fee tier, available plots). Sticky compare-bar.
- **Private rental-income illustrator**, behind owner-portal login (post-purchase) — uses real historic data for that residence type with worked example. Once logged in, the user is no longer a "consumer being marketed an investment".
- **Sublet calendar yield preview**, behind sales-rep CRM only — for the sales rep to walk a prospect through in person, with the disclosure script logged.

## 9. Sales Rep Tools / CRM Integration

**No off-the-shelf UK holiday-park lodge-sales CRM** surfaced in research — the industry runs on generic CRMs (Pipedrive, HubSpot, Salesforce). Goodlife Lodge Company, Quickmove and the larger operators are presumed to be on Salesforce/HubSpot based on form behaviour.

**Recommended for Whalesborough:**
- **HubSpot Sales Hub** (or Pipedrive) as system of record
- **Web → CRM webhook**: brochure-request, viewing-booking, plot-map "interested" all push enriched leads (budget band, timeline, preferred residence, plot ID) into the deal pipeline
- **Bidirectional plot-state sync**: when a plot moves to "Reserved" in the CRM, the website map updates within seconds (Postgres row-level state + Realtime channel, or webhook → revalidate)
- **Sales-rep mobile view** for in-park viewings: plot map on tablet, residence detail, deposit-collection (Stripe terminal), e-sign reservation agreement
- **Compliance log**: every prospect interaction recording which version of the risk panel was shown (page version-stamp), AML expectations explainer viewed Y/N, brochure version sent
- **Email/SMS routing via Resend / Twilio** with separate consent surfaces (PECR — see `uk-compliance-security.md` §1; lodge-sales marketing ≠ holiday-booking soft-opt-in)
- **AML upload** — owner-portal upload of ID and proof-of-funds to solicitor, encrypted at rest; status visible to rep but not the docs

## 10. Pricing Strategy Observations

**Three patterns in the market:**

1. **Full asking price displayed** — Gull Rocks Beach (£299,000 / £449,000 etc.), justlodges listings. **Best for trust.** Tells the buyer this isn't a teaser.
2. **"From £X"** — Bude Coastal Resort (from £195k/£345k), Luxury Lodges Residences (from £345k), Away Resorts (from £159,995), Park Holidays (from £10,995). **Acceptable only if the lowest-priced unit genuinely exists and is purchasable.** ASA precedent on misleading "from" pricing is well-established and any qualifier ("subject to availability", "selected pitches") must be visible at the same point.
3. **Range £X–£Y** — Away Resorts (£295k–£525k lodge tier). **Best for premium-tier portfolios** where there's genuine variation and the buyer needs to self-qualify.

**Recommendation for Whalesborough:**
- **Tier landing page** (`02-lodge-sales`): show range "£X–£Y" per tier (cottage / lodge / villa).
- **Tier detail page** (e.g. `04-luxury-villas`): "From £X" with a visible "lowest current price" plot link.
- **Residence product page**: **full asking price** for each specific residence/plot. No teasing.
- All prices accompanied by "plus annual site fee of £X, utilities metered, see [Costs](07-the-costs) for the full breakdown".
- No "exclusive of VAT" footnotes — be explicit either way; with lodges, VAT depends on the structure of the sale (chattel vs land element).

## 11. Legal & Regulatory Framework — Quick Reference

(Cross-references `uk-compliance-security.md` for full treatment.)

- **FCA** — holiday-lodge "investment schemes" that pool returns can be Unregulated Collective Investment Schemes; promoting them without authorisation is a criminal offence (FSMA s.21). Whalesborough sells lodges as **lifestyle assets with optional managed sublet** — not as investments. Specialist FCA legal review of all copy is mandatory before go-live.
- **ASA / CAP Code §14** (Financial products) — quoted prices must include non-optional fees; financial offers must be understandable; cannot exploit credulity. Luxury Lodge Estates ruling (A24-1256125, 2025) is the precedent.
- **Mobile Homes Act 1983** — generally **does not apply** to lodges on sites with planning permission expressed for holiday use only. Pitch agreements are personal contracts; existing licences may not bind a new park owner. Disclose this on `05-buyers-guide` and `10-what-to-consider`.
- **Caravan Sites and Control of Development Act 1968** — establishes the site-licence regime; lodges meeting the size definition can be caravans for legal purposes.
- **BS 3632:2023** — the British Standard for residential park homes / year-round lodges. Updated 2023 (heat-loss, ventilation, vapour control, fire). Lodges meeting BS 3632:2023 are suitable for 12-month occupation **subject to the site licence**. Whalesborough's planning permission almost certainly restricts to 11-month holiday occupation — disclose openly.
- **125-year licence / lease** — common premium-tier tenure. The clock starts at first sale; with each onward sale the remaining term reduces. Value implications: short-lease lodges depreciate faster. Bude Residences claim "up to 950 years" — exceptional.
- **AML / Money Laundering Regulations 2017** — solicitor must verify ID, source of funds, and source of wealth. Three-stage process. Buyers should expect to provide bank statements, ID, and a written source-of-funds narrative. Lodge purchases over £150k generally trigger enhanced due diligence at the solicitor. Whalesborough's site can pre-warn buyers to shorten the deal.
- **Equality Act 2010 / WCAG 2.2 AA** — the public lodge-sales pages must hit AA. The owner portal post-purchase must hit AA too.
- **DUAA 2025 / PECR** — lodge-sales marketing is **not** "similar product" to a holiday booking and needs explicit, separately-collected consent. Wire the brochure form to a separate consent surface from any holiday-booking flow.
- **Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013** — high-value sales; cooling-off-rights position depends on whether the contract is on the seller's premises. Document this on the reservation contract.
