## 8. Try.be Booking Flow

The booking platform is **Trybe** (`thewclub.try.be`), a UK-built spa/leisure SaaS. The flow on the current public site:

**Step 1 — Browse catalogue (`/`)**
- Single grid of items (treatments + spa days + packages) with image, name, "From £X" price, duration ("Lasts 1 hour 30 minutes").
- Filters available: **date picker** + **type filter** ("Any date" / "Any type") at top, plus inline **search**.

**Step 2 — Item detail page (`/items/<id>/<slug>`)**
- Hero image, item name, duration line.
- Long-form description (HTML-formatted; markdown / bold copy carrying treatment benefits & options).
- "From £X" sticky price block.
- **Guest count** stepper (default `1 guest`).
- **Inline calendar** — Next Month / Month Year scroll; pick a date.
- "**Add to order**" button → "You'll choose the time on the next stage" microcopy.
- For packages (Swim Spa Dine, Teen Pamper, etc.) the CTA is "**Configure package**" → "Customize this package to add it to your order" — i.e. multi-step config (treatment choice, upgrades, dining add-ons).
- "**Interested in upgrading?**" cross-sell row (e.g. Salt & Oil Scrub + Jade Facial promoted on Hot Stone item).
- "**You might also be interested in…**" related-items grid at bottom.

**Step 3 — Time / therapist selection**
(Per microcopy "You'll choose the time on the next stage" — happens after date selection; specific therapist assignment is automatic / handled by Trybe based on availability.)

**Step 4 — Cart / order configuration**
Multi-item orders supported ("Add to order"), with package customisation panels for combo bookings.

**Step 5 — Checkout**
- For online bookings: **payment required at time of booking**.
- For phone bookings: **"Pay by Link" sent — must be paid within 24 hours** or booking is released.
- Accepted: all major credit cards (NOT Amex), valid in-date gift vouchers, cash (in person only).
- Membership renewals: monthly Direct Debit / card on file via Trybe's recurring payments.

**Cookie banner:** "We use cookies to provide necessary website functionality, improve your experience and analyze our traffic. Accept all / Cookie settings." (Granular consent supported.)

### Try.be platform features observed
- **Service catalogue** with category filters, search, date filter
- **Inline calendar booking** with multi-stage flow
- **Package configurator** (multi-treatment, choice-of-X within package)
- **Cross-sell / upgrade prompts** on item pages
- **Gift voucher e-commerce** — separate `/vouchers` storefront with monetary + experience vouchers, post-purchase delivery (digital PDF assumed)
- **Membership management** — `/memberships` exists; signup goes through email + payment-link flow
- **Stand-alone bookings for non-spa services** (e.g. Wellness Check at £35) — the platform supports any fixed-fee timed service
- **No on-platform retail e-commerce** for physical products
- **No native class / schedule / drop-in booking** (yoga, group fitness etc. not present on Try.be)
- **No customer account portal** visible publicly (manage bookings, see history, store payment methods — these likely live behind login)

## SUMMARY — for the £500k Whalesborough app build

**Spa scope today.** W Club operates a 4-line treatment menu (Gaia Rituals, Facials, Body & Massage, Hands & Feet) — **~30 distinct SKUs** across 45 / 60 / 90 / 120-min slots, weekday/weekend dual pricing, plus **14 spa-day packages** (3–6 hr experiences anchored to thermal facilities + treatment + optional dining at The Weir). Three membership tiers (Gym £47, Full Single £77, Full Couples £132) — all on waiting list, with £25 admin fee and 1-month notice. Gift vouchers in seven monetary values plus 11 experience SKUs. Cancellation policy is strict: 48 hr (72 hr for groups ≥4), full charge for no-show or late cancel.

**What's missing from the current stack.** No on-page reviews, no native apps, no member portal visible, no class schedule (zero yoga / group fitness despite gym), no retail e-commerce for Gaia products, no integration between spa (Try.be) / accommodation (Landal) / dining (no booking system at all). Trust signals limited to a single 5/5 Google badge. Treatment history, therapist profiles, pre-treatment consultation forms, push reminders, loyalty, referrals, in-app concierge — all absent. The booking flow itself is competent but generic — guests can't pick their therapist, can't see the full resort calendar, and offers like "Buy One Get One Half Price" funnel out to phone only.

**What we need to build to exceed best-in-class.** Native iOS/Android with unified resort calendar (spa + dining + accommodation in one cart); rich therapist profiles + therapist-of-choice booking; per-guest treatment history and wellness journal; pre-arrival digital consultation forms; push-notification appointment reminders + smart upsells; loyalty programme tied to membership and visit cadence; class schedule with drop-in / pre-pay / waitlist (yoga, aqua, circuits); native Gaia retail e-commerce with skincare regimen recommendations; review collection + on-page social proof; in-app concierge chat for offers and bespoke packages; Apple/Google Wallet passes; full UK GDPR/DUAA-2025 compliance; analytics dashboard exposing spa utilisation, ARPU per guest, package mix, and member retention — none of which Try.be surfaces today.
