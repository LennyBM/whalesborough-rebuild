## 2. Top UX Patterns Observed (across the field)

1. **Date-first search with sticky availability strip** — the entire industry has converged on date+party as the entry filter, with everything else as progressive disclosure.
2. **Calendar-view itinerary post-booking** (Center Parcs, Haven, Landal) — guests see confirmed activities (blue) vs. basket (orange) in a unified timeline.
3. **Party-member self-service** — Center Parcs lets each adult on a booking book their own activities/restaurants from their own logged-in view, sharing an itinerary.
4. **Digital key + check-in skip** (Landal) — share with travelling party 4 days ahead. Industry-leading.
5. **Pre-arrival extras shop** — hampers, dog treats, celebration packages, sandwiches-for-tomorrow (Landal), breakfast hampers (Forest Holidays).
6. **In-resort table-side QR order to home/table** (Haven, Bluestone, Landal).
7. **Numbered audio-guide estate map** (The Newt) — turns the map into a tour, not just wayfinding.
8. **Members-only rates revealed on login** (The Newt, Soho House) — a clean conversion mechanic for the ownership tier.
9. **Saved search profiles** (Sykes) — multiple parties / occasions per account.
10. **Pet-as-first-class-citizen** (Hoseasons "Pets Free of Charge" filter, Sykes enclosed-garden filter).
11. **Real-time availability for next 7 days** (Bluestone) — reduces the cognitive overhead of activity planning.
12. **Connected ordering ecosystem** — food, activity, spa, restaurant in one cart/account (Center Parcs, Landal, Away).

## 3. Lodge Sales Journey Patterns (£400k+ ownership)

What other parks do that Whalesborough should emulate:

- **Tier 1 — Marketing site separate from holiday site**: Park Holidays runs `myholidayparks.com` for investment, distinct from `parkholidays.com` for booking. Forest Holidays and Center Parcs hide ownership behind a separate sales contact form, *not* the booking flow.
- **Tier 2 — Rental guarantee documentation**: Center Parcs offers a legally enforceable rental guarantee based on past occupancy. This is *the* trust signal that closes high-value buyers.
- **Tier 3 — Fractional ownership option**: Park Holidays offers outright vs. fractional. HPB ran £380M of fractional via Holiday Points (1 point = £1 invested = annual usage value).
- **Tier 4 — Personal-use + rental hybrid**: Industry standard is "5 weeks personal + the rest is sub-let income at agreed split."
- **Tier 5 — Owner portal**: Park Holidays `owners.parkholidays.com` is the published benchmark — bills, news bulletins, owner FAQs, events calendar, owner-priority rewards card. Parkdean similar.
- **Tier 6 — Owner rewards (not just income)**: VIP events, food/drink discounts, cashback cards, priority booking on other estate amenities.
- **What's missing across all operators**: A transparent, real-time **rental income dashboard** with occupancy %, gross/net split, comparative park-average, year-on-year trend. Park Holidays only offers an annual statement. *This is a clear Whalesborough opportunity.*

## 4. Activity Booking Patterns

- **Filter dimensions used industry-wide**: activity type, age range, accompaniment, start time, indoor/outdoor, weather-dependent.
- **Capacity model**: 7-day rolling availability (Bluestone) for fast-moving slots; longer horizon for headline experiences.
- **Tiered access**: "Visible to all" vs "visible to booked guests only" (Center Parcs).
- **Multi-service sequential booking** (modern spa software like Trybe/Zenoti): full spa-day flow with staggered start times, room assignment per service, therapist matching, system resolves dependencies. Whalesborough's spa partner Try.be does this natively.
- **Bookable activity types observed**: surf/SUP lessons, archery, laser tag, alpaca walks (£30 incl refreshments, goodie bag — Longthorns Farm benchmark), high ropes, bike hire, junior Land Rovers, forest-ranger sessions, in-cabin spa, kayaking, foraging, bushcraft, beach school, painting/lino/clay workshops, cookery, wine tasting.
- **Activity cross-sell timing**: Soho House sends an "activities email" *a few weeks before arrival* — this is the highest-conversion pre-arrival touchpoint observed.

## 5. Estate Map UX Patterns

- **The Newt (gold standard)**: Interactive map of estate; "Estate" tab shows areas, restaurants, shops, gardens, amenities. Click "Garden tour" for audio + written narration on a numbered route.
- **Center Parcs**: Village map embedded in app with way-finding from your lodge to indoor pool/restaurant/spa.
- **Haven**: Interactive park map with venue opening times overlay.
- **Landal**: In-app park map with park facilities + food + drink + entertainment layered on.
- **Common missing element**: Live status overlay (queue length, weather impact on outdoor activities, "spaces left today" for unbooked drop-ins). *Whalesborough opportunity.*

## 6. Owner Portal Patterns

Standard feature set seen across Park Holidays / Parkdean / Center Parcs investor schemes:

- My Account (login, account details, finance)
- Bill payment portal
- News bulletins / park updates
- Event calendar (owner-exclusive events)
- Owner FAQs
- Owner-priority rewards card (cashback, discounts, cashless payments)
- Letting-scheme enrolment toggle
- Annual rental statement (PDF)

**Conspicuously absent across operators** (Whalesborough first-mover opportunity):
- Real-time rental income dashboard
- Live occupancy calendar with comparative-park benchmarks
- Per-stay guest review feed
- Maintenance ticket portal + response SLAs
- Concierge add-on revenue split visibility
- Transparent annual P&L (site fees, management, marketing, net to owner)
- Resale marketplace integrated with the owner portal
- API/feed export for owner's accountant
- Investor reporting suitable for SPV / portfolio ownership

## 7. Definitive Feature List for Whalesborough Modules

### A. Accommodation Booking Module
1. Date + party + pet + accessibility entry filter
2. Progressive filters: lodge tier, view, hot-tub, dog-friendly, EV charging, accessible, multi-gen-adjacent
3. Property detail: 360 virtual tour (Matterport-grade), photo gallery (cover + interior + view + floor plan), amenities matrix, sleeps-N detail, distance-to-X chips (spa, restaurant, beach)
4. Interactive estate map showing lodge position before booking
5. Live availability calendar with price-per-night transparency
6. Adjoining-lodge "book both" option (multi-gen)
7. Cross-sell drawer on confirmation: restaurant reservation, spa slots, activity bundle, hamper, dog welcome pack, EV charging credit
8. Members-only rate reveal on login (owner / repeat-guest tier)
9. Saved search profiles (Sykes pattern)
10. Pet-as-guest UX: pet name, breed, treat preference at booking

### B. Activity Booking Module
11. Calendar-view itinerary, party-member self-service (Center Parcs pattern)
12. 7-day rolling availability for fast-movers + 12-week for headline experiences
13. Filters: type, age, indoor/outdoor, accompaniment, start time, weather-resilience
14. Multi-service sequential booking (spa-day flow with staggered starts, room assignment, therapist matching — Trybe-equivalent)
15. Tiered access: "available to all" vs "booked-guests-only" vs "owner-exclusive"
16. Pre-arrival activities email cadence (3 weeks out — Soho House timing)
17. Live status overlay on estate map (queue, weather, spaces-left)
18. In-stay ordering: table-side QR, in-lodge delivery, restaurant reservations, sandwiches-for-tomorrow
19. Digital key (Landal pattern), shareable to party 4 days pre-arrival

### C. Lodge Sales / Ownership Module
20. Separate sales site (do not contaminate booking flow)
21. Investment calculator: purchase price, predicted gross, costs, net return (with comparable-park data)
22. Rental guarantee documentation prominent
23. Outright vs fractional option selector
24. Sales journey: enquire → virtual tour → site visit → reservation deposit → contract
25. **Owner portal (the differentiator)**:
    - Real-time rental income dashboard (gross, net, YoY, park-average benchmark)
    - Live occupancy calendar
    - Per-stay guest reviews
    - Maintenance ticket portal with SLA tracking
    - Concierge add-on revenue split visibility
    - Annual P&L with downloadable accountant feed
    - Owner-exclusive event booking
    - Resale marketplace
    - Owner rewards card linked to all estate amenities

## 8. First-Mover Opportunities (no Cornwall competitor doing these in May 2026)

Cross-referencing Carbis Bay, Bedruthan, St Moritz, Retallack, Watergate Bay, Boveys + the Landal/Center Parcs/Forest Holidays standard:

1. **Landal-grade digital key + share-to-party** — no Cornwall operator offers this; reception-skip is the single biggest perceived-luxury gain.
2. **The Newt-grade audio-guided estate map** — none of Carbis Bay, Watergate Bay, Bedruthan have an interactive estate app. A numbered audio tour of Whalesborough's grounds (woodland, lake, alpacas, kitchen garden) would be unique on the Cornish coast.
3. **Real-time rental income dashboard for lodge owners** — Park Holidays/Center Parcs only do annual statements. Live dashboard is a category-leading move.
4. **Party-member self-service activity booking** (Center Parcs pattern) — no Cornwall resort offers this; current state is one-person-books-everything.
5. **Multi-service sequential spa-day booking** (Trybe is already in place at Whalesborough — surface its power in the guest UI).
6. **Pre-arrival activities email at +3 weeks** with personalised recommendations (Soho House pattern, mass-market-applied).
7. **Pet-as-guest profile with treat preference, walking route map, dog-spa booking** — Hoseasons leads on filters but no resort yet treats pets as named guests.
8. **Live "spaces left today" overlay on activity map** — turns the in-stay app into an exploration tool, not just a booking tool.
9. **Resale marketplace inside the owner portal** — currently fragmented across holiday-park-resale.co.uk; integrated would lock owners into the ecosystem.
10. **Sustainability passport per stay** — EV credit, carbon-of-travel-offset, local-sourcing index, estate-grown produce on plate this week. No Cornwall competitor doing this transparently.
