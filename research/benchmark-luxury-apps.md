## 3. Best UX Patterns Observed

1. **Soho House — Location-aware homepage.** App detects which House you've "checked in" to and surfaces content (today's events, available tables, pool slots) for that location only. Removes cognitive load. **Pattern for Whalesborough:** when guest geofences onto the estate, app surfaces *today's* on-estate activities, weather, spa availability, supper club menu — not the global homepage.

2. **Four Seasons — Chat as single interaction surface.** All requests (housekeeping, dining, laundry, wake-up calls, pillow swap) confirmed via chat thread, not separate forms. 90-second SLA. Pattern: don't build 14 separate forms — build one chat thread with shortcut chips ("Order coffee", "More towels", "Walk my dog").

3. **The Pig — Bundle the value prop into the booking flow.** "Restaurant with rooms" means a guest who can't simultaneously book a table and a room will defect. Their booking flow surfaces table availability inside the room booking confirmation. Pattern: Whalesborough's "farm with rooms with spa" needs the booking flow to surface restaurant table + spa slot + activity at the moment of room confirmation, not 3 emails later.

4. **Watergate Bay — Local-data layer.** Tide times, surf reports, weather. The guest opens the app for a *reason* other than booking. App becomes daily-use, not one-use. Pattern: Whalesborough should layer farm-shop opening, harvest calendar, dog walks by weather, today's tide for nearby coast, badger watch booking.

5. **Six Senses — Pre-stay preference questionnaire feels like service, not data capture.** Asked in a curated, story-led tone ("how do you want your room to feel when you walk in?"), not a form. Pattern: copy the tone exactly. Whalesborough should ask about dog name, room scent, drink preference in conversational copy.

6. **Mandarin Oriental — Loyalty tiers without points.** Fans of M.O. Silk/Pearl is invitation + behaviour-based, not points-accumulated. Feels exclusive even at the entry tier. Pattern: Whalesborough should NOT do a points loyalty programme — it cheapens. Do invitation-based "Farm Friends" / "Estate Circle" tiering.

7. **Soho House — House Pay's tab flow.** Open tab → see live bill → split → tip → digital receipt. Single button settle. Pattern: every guest-facing F&B touchpoint at Whalesborough should accept House Pay-style mobile-tab payment. Removes 3-5 minutes from every restaurant visit.

8. **Hilton — Digital key sharing with up to 4 devices, plus physical keys still work simultaneously.** Acknowledges that one guest is rarely alone. Pattern: Whalesborough family bookings = parents + kids + dog-walker, all need access. Don't force the "primary guest only" model.

9. **COMO Shambhala — Tiered content depth (10/30/60-min sessions).** Same wellness instructor available in 10 minutes (quick refresh) or 60 minutes (deep practice). Pattern: any content library in the Whalesborough app should be time-tiered to match guest mood.

10. **Soneva — Barefoot Guardian model.** Each villa has a dedicated human, NOT replaced by app — app *augments* their service. Pattern: don't position app as replacement for service. Position as accelerator of service. (Especially important given Whalesborough's hospitality DNA.)

## 7. Strategic Read

Whalesborough's £500k budget at a 30-unit Cornwall resort is roughly the sweet spot of the Six Senses La Sagesse (OKKAMI) and Watergate Bay deployments — but with deliberately better product. The dominant insight from the matrix is that **the UK rural-luxury set has barely entered native mobile.** Heckfield Place is actively anti-digital. Pig, Beaverbrook, Chewton Glen, Tresanton, St Moritz, Carbis Bay, Coworth Park all run on web booking widgets + third-party booking platforms (SevenRooms etc.). Only Watergate Bay has an app, and reviews indicate it's broken on Android.

This means Whalesborough's app does not need to compete with Aman or Soho House — it needs to deliver the Four-Seasons-grade chat + Six-Senses-grade preference profile + The-Pig-grade bundle booking + Hilton-grade key sharing + the missing dog/farm/tide/Cornwall-specific layer, in one product, on day one. That is genuinely achievable on £500k with the right partner stack (OKKAMI or Intelity for core, custom layer for farm/dog/Cornwall, RAG-based AI concierge).

The features no one will copy quickly: farm-to-fork tap-to-trace, dog concierge, estate trail map with offline AR, Cornwall Wildlife Trust 1% give-back. These four are the moat.
