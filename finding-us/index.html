Driving Directions

Step by step from the major routes.

From the M5 / South East

Via the A361 North Devon Link Road

  1. Leave the M5 at Junction 27 (Tiverton) and take the A361 westbound towards Barnstaple.
  2. At Barnstaple, take the A39 Atlantic Highway heading south-west towards Bude.
  3. Continue on the A39 through Camelford and Camelford. Pass through Stratton on the outskirts of Bude.
  4. After Stratton, take the right turn signed Marhamchurch. Follow the road into the village.
  5. Continue through Marhamchurch and follow the brown tourist signs for Whalesborough Farm Resort.

From the A30 / South West

Via the A39 from Bodmin

  1. From the A30, join the A39 at Camelford heading north towards Bude.
  2. Continue north on the A39 through Wainhouse Corner and Coppathorne.
  3. As you approach Bude, look for the sign for Marhamchurch on your left.
  4. Turn left into Marhamchurch and pass through the village.
  5. Follow the brown tourist signs for Whalesborough Farm Resort to reach the entrance.

By Train

Exeter or Bodmin Parkway

The nearest main line station is Exeter St Davids. From there, take a connecting service to Okehampton or Bodmin Parkway, then a taxi or hire car for the final leg to Bude. A taxi from Exeter to Whalesborough takes approximately 1 hour 15 minutes. We recommend pre-booking a local taxi through Bude Taxis on 01288 352202.

By Coach

National Express to Bude

National Express and Stagecoach operate services to Bude from London and major cities. From Bude bus station, Whalesborough is approximately 3 miles — a short taxi ride. Taxis meet coaches on arrival at Bude. Pre-booking a taxi is recommended in peak season.

EV Charging

4 Charge Points On Site

Whalesborough has 4 electric vehicle charging points available on site for guests. If you are travelling by EV and need to plan charging stops en route, there are rapid chargers in Barnstaple, Launceston, and Bude town centre.
