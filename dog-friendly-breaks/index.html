The Whalesborough Welcome

Your dog is as welcome
as you are

We believe that your dog's stay at Whalesborough should be as relaxing and enjoyable as yours. So we've built that belief into every single detail.

From luxury welcome packs waiting on arrival to dog showers for when they've found the muddiest field on the estate — we've made sure that having your dog with you feels entirely uncomplicated.

Six Reasons to Bring Your Dog

Everything considered. Nothing forgotten.

01

428 Acres to Explore

Long walks, open countryside, fresh air, and fields big enough for any dog to find their own adventure. Our private estate offers more space than most dogs ever encounter in one place.

02

Luxury Pooch Welcome Packs

Waiting in your cottage on arrival. Everything your dog needs to feel at home from day one — treats, essentials, and the kind of welcome that tells you we've thought this through.

03

Dog Showers

Whether they've been swimming at the beach or rolled through every muddy field on the estate, our dog shower facilities mean they come home clean. A game-changer for cottage stays.

04

Enclosed Private Gardens

All but one of our cottages have private enclosed gardens. Let your dog out without a lead, without the anxiety — and enjoy your morning coffee in peace.

05

Large Indoor Dog Crates

Every property has large dog crates for your dog to relax in when you step out. So you can visit the spa or restaurant without worrying about what's happening back at the cottage.

06

Luxury Soft Furnishings

Blankets, sofa covers, and throws for all soft furnishings. Because your dog deserves to be comfortable — and you deserve not to worry about the upholstery.

Nearby Beaches

Know before you go.

North Cornwall has some of the finest dog-friendly beaches in England — but restrictions vary by season. Use this guide when planning your walks.

Year-Round Dog Friendly
Sandymouth Beach
Wild, unspoilt National Trust cove. Dogs welcome all year, no restrictions. Excellent rock pools.
6 min drive
Black Rock, Widemouth Bay
The northern section of Widemouth Bay. Year-round access for dogs. Direct walk from the estate via SWCP.
10 min walk
Duckpool Beach
Remote, pebbly cove near Morwenstow. Year-round dog access. Rugged and rarely crowded.
12 min drive
Crooklets Beach (north section)
Dogs permitted year-round on the northern end of Crooklets, beyond the surf school.
8 min drive
Northcott Mouth
Quiet beach north of Bude. No seasonal restrictions. Excellent for winter walks with the dogs.
10 min drive
Seasonal Restrictions (May–Sept)
Summerleaze Beach, Bude
Dogs banned 10am–6pm May to September on the main beach. Permitted outside these hours and year-round on the far ends.
8 min drive
Crooklets Beach (main)
Central section has seasonal restrictions May to September. Dogs welcome before 10am and after 6pm.
8 min drive
Widemouth Bay (south section)
The popular southern surfing area has summer restrictions on the main bathing zone. North section (Black Rock) remains unrestricted.
10 min walk
Bude Sea Pool area
No dogs in the tidal pool itself. Dogs permitted on the surrounding rocks and paths year-round.
10 min drive

Restrictions are set by local councils and may change. Always check locally before visiting. Information correct as of 2026 season.
