The Challenge

Where Most Resort Websites Fall Short

Almost every luxury resort in Cornwall shares the same weaknesses. Template designs, no hidden data for Google to read, no strategy for AI search, and booking links buried three clicks deep.

✗

Template-based designs

Squarespace and WordPress themes that look like every other hotel in Cornwall. No brand differentiation, no editorial quality — just a booking engine with wallpaper.

✗

Zero structured data (hidden labels for Google)

No behind-the-scenes markup means Google can't show the rich extras — star ratings, FAQs, price ranges, availability. Their listings look plain while others stand out.

✗

No AI search strategy

Not optimised for AI-powered search. When someone asks ChatGPT, Siri, or Google's AI for "best luxury resort near Bude", competitors have nothing structured for the AI to cite.

✗

Poor speed & performance (Core Web Vitals)

Slow loading, content jumping around as the page loads, failing Google's speed tests. Bloated code and unoptimised images are costing them rankings every day.

✗

Booking buried 3+ clicks deep

No quick way to check dates or call to book from the homepage. Guests have to hunt through menus to find availability — and most give up.

✗

No accessibility compliance

Missing image descriptions for blind users, no keyboard navigation, no support for users sensitive to motion. Legal exposure under the Equality Act and a reputational risk.

The AI Search Shift

More and more people are skipping Google entirely. They ask ChatGPT "where should I stay near Bude?", tell Siri "find me a luxury resort in Cornwall", or let Gemini plan their holiday. AI can only recommend businesses whose websites give it clear, labelled facts to work with.

02

The Design

Editorial Luxury, Not a Booking Engine

Most resort websites look like booking engines with a background image. The new Whalesborough site is designed to feel like a luxury publication — the kind of experience that makes guests want to stay before they've even seen a price.

Why "Coastal Editorial"?

We named the design system after its purpose: marry the Cornish coastal identity with the layout discipline of editorial magazines. Every page reads like a feature spread, not a product listing.

The Five-Second Test

A guest lands on the homepage, sees a cinematic video of the estate, a Landal date search, and a phone number to call — all above the fold. They can act immediately or keep scrolling into the story.

As Seen In…

Condé Nast Traveller, Tatler, The Times, The Guardian, Cornwall Life — all displayed prominently. This press strip does more for trust than any amount of marketing copy.

Movement With Purpose

Content reveals as you scroll, images layer with depth, grids stagger in. It feels cinematic, not gimmicky — and it all switches off automatically for users who prefer no motion.

Designed to Feel Expensive

Sharp corners, not rounded. Serif headlines, not sans-serif. Generous whitespace, not cramped grids. Every design choice signals that this is a premium resort, not a budget park.

Nobody Else Has This

This isn't a WordPress theme or an agency template. The entire visual system — colours, typography, spacing, animations — was built specifically for Whalesborough and can't be replicated.

The competitors use templates. Whalesborough has a design system that positions it alongside the publications that feature it.
