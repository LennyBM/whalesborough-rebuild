# Destination Content Design Brief: Bude and North Cornwall

This brief outlines the strategic design and content requirements for a digital destination platform focused on Bude and the surrounding North Cornwall coast. It synthesizes technical best practices with local research to provide clear directives for development and asset acquisition.

---

## I. Interactive Elements Required: Local Area Guide

The "Local Area Guide" must function as a high-performance interactive map UI, prioritising mobile responsiveness and ease of navigation.

### 1. Technical Specifications

- **Map Interface:** Utilise a full-screen layout on both desktop and mobile to maximise the visual experience of the North Cornwall coastline.
- **Navigation Logic:** Incorporate standard zoom controls (top left) and intuitive scroll/touch functionality. Implement point clustering to prevent overcrowding in high-density areas like Bude town centre.
- **POI (Point of Interest) Details:** Details for selected locations should appear in a side panel (desktop) or a collapsible full-screen overlay (mobile). Details must include:
  - Distance/proximity to key accommodation hubs (e.g., Widemouth Bay or Bude town centre).
  - Location-specific statistics (e.g., tide times, walking distance, or seasonal dog restrictions).
  - High-quality image gallery for each point.
- **Customisation:** Use branded, themed pins (e.g., surfboard icons for surf schools, paw prints for dog-friendly beaches) and a colour palette of 3–5 brand-aligned colours.

### 2. Mandatory Filters

The UI must allow users to refine their search using the following category-based filters derived from local activity data:

- **Accessibility:** Level paths, transit wheelchair hire locations, and beach sand chair availability (e.g., Summerleaze Beach).
- **Pet-Friendly:** Year-round dog-friendly beaches (e.g., Black Rock, Sandymouth) vs. seasonal ban areas.
- **Family-Friendly:** Safe bathing areas (Bude Sea Pool), playgrounds, and beginner-friendly activity centres.
- **Activity Type:** Watersports (surfing, wakeboarding, rowing), Outdoor Adventures (Segway, SWCP hikes), and Sports (Golf, Fishing).
- **Heritage & Culture:** Historical sites (Bude Castle, Poundstock Gildhouse) and art/craft galleries.
- **Rainy Day Attractions:** Indoor options (Rebel Cinema, Heritage Coast Exhibition, Pottery/Glass workshops).
- **Dining:** Filterable by cuisine (Tapas, Seafood, Sunday Roast, Cream Tea) and features (Sunset views, Dog-friendly indoors).

## II. Content Modules: Wireframe Requirements

Content blocks must follow established usability patterns, such as the F- and Z-layouts, to lead the user through the guest journey effectively.

### 1. Homepage Modules

- **Hero Visual Experience:** A high-definition video or photography carousel showcasing the "experience" of Bude (e.g., the Sea Pool at high tide or surfing at Widemouth Bay).
- **Seasonal "Vibe" Switcher:** A module that updates content based on the four seasons:
  - **Spring:** Focus on wildflowers, early surf lessons, and off-peak rate "smart savings."
  - **Summer:** Focus on festivals (Leopalooza, Bude Carnival), late sunsets, and family attractions.
  - **Autumn:** Focus on the Bude for Food Festival and high-quality Atlantic surf swells.
  - **Winter:** Focus on dramatic coastal walks, cosy pub interiors with real fires, and Christmas events.
- **One-of-a-Kind Promotions:** A highlighted block for exclusive packages and "best deal" offers to drive direct bookings over third-party agents.
- **Regional Quick-Links:** Horizontal scrolling blocks for key sub-destinations: Widemouth Bay, Bude Town, Crackington Haven, and Tamar Lakes.

### 2. Accommodation Page Modules

- **Room/Property Comparison:** Clear tables or blocks distinguishing between room types, including amenities like private hot tubs, enclosed gardens, and EV charging stations.
- **Dog-Friendly Specifics:** A dedicated feature block for pet owners highlighting "Pooch Welcome Packs," on-site dog showers, and soft furnishings (throws/covers).
- **Price Point Transparency:** Inclusion of "Starting from" prices to prevent users from leaving the site to check rates on OTAs.
- **Reservation Specifics:** Immediate visibility of cancellation policies and a "Guest FAQ" accordion to answer common questions regarding parking, check-in, and local facilities.

## III. Asset Wishlist: Photography and Video

High-quality visuals are the primary driver of engagement for destination websites. Assets must be "on brand" and distinguish the region from competitors.

### 1. Aerial and Panoramic Footage

- **Bude Sea Pool:** Dramatic drone footage at high tide showing the sea washing into the pool.
- **South West Coast Path (SWCP):** Wide shots of the rugged cliffs at Phillips's Point and the views over Whalesborough and Widemouth Bay.
- **Bude Canal:** Low-altitude sweeps of the canal towpath, the sea lock, and the historical Wharf buildings.
- **Hartland Heritage Coast:** Panoramic views of the 60-mile coastline stretching toward Exmoor.

### 2. Lifestyle and Activity Assets

- **The "Cornish Cream Tea":** Close-up shots of a cream tea at a location like The Rectory Tea Rooms *(Note: jam must be applied before the cream)*.
- **Sunset Dining:** Candid shots of guests at The Beach House or Bay View Inn watching the sun set over the Atlantic.
- **Action Sports:** Surfers at Widemouth Bay, rowing boats on the canal, and cyclists on the Camel Trail.
- **Unique Experiences:** Glass art creation at Teylu Glass/Belle Craft, pottery throwing, and the Ocean Soul mobile sauna at Crooklets Beach.

### 3. Property and Amenity Assets

- **Pet Amenities:** High-quality photos of dogs using on-site dog showers and relaxing in enclosed private gardens.
- **Sustainable Features:** Imagery of EV chargers, organic market gardens, and re-wilding projects (e.g., sunflowers and bird boxes).
- **Accessibility in Action:** Wheelchair-friendly paths at Bude Castle or level access points at Summerleaze Beach.
- **Interiors:** Wide-angle shots of accommodation interiors (cottages, Arvor suites) focusing on "luxury" elements like log fires and high-end kitchens.

### 4. Seasonal Imagery

- **Winter:** "Storm watching" photography showing the surf pounding the breakwater.
- **Spring:** Macro shots of wildflowers in the Bude Marshes or hedgerows.
- **Summer:** Vibrant images of Summerleaze beach huts and the Bude Jazz Festival.
