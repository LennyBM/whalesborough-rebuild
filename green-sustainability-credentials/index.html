The Cleaner Seas Initiative

The first UK resort with microplastic filters on every washing machine

A single wash releases up to 700,000 microfibres into the water system — and from there, into the sea. We installed Cleaner Seas microplastic filters on the washing machines of all 26 cottages.

No other holiday resort in the UK had done this before we did.

The microfibres we collect are harvested and recycled into future products. We use Ocean Saver plant-based cleaning products throughout — plastic-free, effective, and kind to the same water we're protecting.

A Living Estate

Wildflower meadows.
Active ecosystems.
Land that gives back.

We’ve placed bird boxes throughout, established organic market garden operations, and protected the hedgerows that frame our 428 acres. The estate is not just a backdrop — it’s an active ecosystem.

Wildflower meadows feed both wildlife and the kitchen. Working farmland sits alongside protected habitat. The land does its job — for guests, for wildlife, for the long term.

We manage 428 acres with biodiversity as the outcome, not a by-product.

Green Energy

Biomass district heating.
Cleaner Seas filters.
Real change at source.

A biomass boiler provides district heating across the estate. Air source heat pumps heat our pools. Cleaner Seas microplastic filters run on every cottage washing machine.

Our goal: full energy self-sufficiency. We are well on the way.

Four super-fast EV charging points are available on-site, managed via app. A network of footpaths connects to the Bude Canal towpath and Sustrans cycle tracks for guests who want to arrive — and explore — without a car.

Transparency · Our Roadmap

What we've done, what we're doing, and what comes next

We don't just report on achievements. We publish our ongoing commitments, so you can hold us to them.

Complete

Microplastic filters across all 26 cottages

UK-first installation of Cleaner Seas filters across every cottage washing machine on the estate. Microfibres are harvested and recycled — not discharged into coastal waters.

Complete

Biomass district heating across the estate

Biomass district heating combined with air source heat pumps provides clean energy across the entire estate. Grid dependency significantly reduced.

Complete

biomass district heating and wildflower habitat

Working in partnership with local conservation partners, we've transformed former agricultural margins into thriving wildlife corridors, wildflower meadows, and managed woodland.

In Progress

Full energy self-sufficiency by 2027

We're currently at 65% self-sufficiency. Additional solar capacity across cottage rooflines and a battery storage system are planned to close the gap.

In Progress

Annual biodiversity audit publication

We've commissioned ongoing species surveys across the estate. Results will be published annually — invertebrate counts, bird nesting data, hedgerow health — making our impact visible and verifiable.

Planned · 2026

EV charging expansion + cycle hire scheme

Expanding from 4 to 12 EV charging bays, plus partnering with a local cycle hire operator to offer guest bike hire direct from reception — making car-free exploration genuinely possible.

Questions

Frequently asked.

What sustainability awards has Whalesborough Farm Resort won?

Whalesborough Farm Resort & Spa won the Gold Award at the Cornwall Tourism Awards 2024/25 in the category of Ethical, Responsible & Sustainable Tourism. The resort also holds VisitEngland 5-Star Gold and VisitBritain 5-Star Gold ratings across multiple properties.

Is Whalesborough Farm Resort eco-friendly?

Yes. Whalesborough Farm Resort & Spa is one of the UK's most sustainability-focused luxury resorts. Key credentials include on-site renewable energy infrastructure (biomass boiler, air source heat pump for pool heating, 4 EV charging points), organic market garden farming, and a target of full energy self-sufficiency. The resort holds a Gold Award from Cornwall Tourism Awards 2024/25 for sustainable tourism.

What are Cleaner Seas microplastic filters and does Whalesborough use them?

Whalesborough Farm Resort is the first holiday resort in the UK to fit Cleaner Seas microplastic filters on every cottage washing machine. Each filter captures approximately 700,000 microfibres per wash cycle, preventing them from entering the water system and ultimately the sea. This initiative protects the local marine environment around the North Cornwall coastline near Bude.

How does Whalesborough Farm generate its energy?

Whalesborough Farm Resort generates green energy through on-site renewable infrastructure, including a biomass boiler and air source heat pump technology used to heat the spa pools. The resort is targeting full energy self-sufficiency as part of its long-term sustainability programme.

How does Whalesborough support biodiversity?

Whalesborough Farm Resort supports local wildlife and biodiversity across its 428-acre working Cornish estate through wildflower meadows, hedgerow management, and habitat protection. 50 new acres of woodland have been planted since 2020, and 9,000 sunflowers are planted annually across estate wildflower zones. The resort holds the Gold Award from the Cornwall Tourism Awards 2024/25 for Ethical, Responsible & Sustainable Tourism.
