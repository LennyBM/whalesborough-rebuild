The space

Moleyns is a professionally designed semi-detached new build set within the heart of the resort. The open-plan ground floor centres on a statement leather corner sofa — with built-in lounger and separate two-seater — positioned to face both the contemporary wood burner and a 42" LCD TV. Bosch appliances and an American fridge-freezer keep the kitchen a pleasure to cook in, while bifold doors bring the outside in. Three bedrooms — each with its own balcony — give the cottage a genuine sense of space for six.

Directly connected to the W Club, Moleyns guests enjoy some of the easiest resort access of any cottage on the estate. The indoor pool sits at a constant 29–30°C year-round, the outdoor pool opens seasonally at 26°C, and sauna, steam room and Technogym gym are all included. The Weir Restaurant is a short stroll away for relaxed meals with estate views. Dogs are welcome — the grounds and coastal trails start almost at the door, making this equally brilliant for four-legged guests.

The estate & the coast

Whalesborough is a 428-acre working estate on the North Cornwall coast, a few minutes from the surf town of Bude and the wide golden sands of Widemouth Bay. Private trails run from the cottage door across the estate and down toward the sea, and the South West Coast Path is close in both directions — this is the quieter, less-trodden coast: real Cornwall, without the August queues.

At the heart of the estate, the W Club spa, indoor and outdoor heated pools and The Weir Restaurant are all yours to enjoy. Bude has everything you need — proper coffee, good pubs and a chip shop or two worth the walk — while Tintagel, Boscastle and Padstow are all an easy drive when you fancy a day out.
