### Heading: "Hitting the coast"

### Description
"We're so lucky to have the North Cornwall Coastline right on our doorstep which offers our guests miles and miles of breathtaking scenery, wildlife, and activities."

### South West Coast Path (North Route)
"Walkers will be delighted to hear that we're just a few minutes away from the South West Coast Path which is the best way to discover the hidden coves and rare wildlife that North Cornwall has to offer!"
Recommended beaches: Summerleaze, Crooklets, Northcott, Sandymouth, plus Hartland Heritage Coast

### Hartland Heritage Coast
Distance: 5 miles away
"This 60-mile long route is full of stunning scenery, rare wildlife, shipwrecks, sandy beaches, quaint villages and so much more!"
Features Heritage Coast Exhibition at Visitor Centre in Bude.

### South West Coast Path (South Route)
"The breath-taking trail spans 630 miles from Dorset to Somerset, and is a great opportunity to discover local beauty points, secret beaches and rare wildlife."
Recommended: Water Cove, Tom's Cove, Widemouth Bay, Black Rock Beach, Crackington Haven

### Local Beaches
"At Whalesborough we're so lucky to be totally surrounded by some of the UK's most popular, and most breathtaking beaches."
Highlighted: Millook, Water Cove, Widemouth, Summerleaze

### The Camel Trail
"One of the most popular cycling and walking trails in the country; the Camel Trail takes you from Bodmin, through Wenford Bridge and over to Padstow, offering 18 miles of stunning coastal and countryside scenery."

## EXPLORING CORNWALL — /exploring-cornwall

### Heading: "Exploring Cornwall"

### Featured Destinations

**Bude**
"The area has a laid-back charm that hasn't been spoiled by development" and was "officially the sunniest place in the UK in 2013." Historic architecture from 16th century through Victorian periods.

**Boscastle** — 13 miles away
"Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty." Boat trips, seals year-round, puffins during breeding season, quaint harbour.

**Tintagel** — under 30 minutes
Castle ruins on dramatic headland. High footbridge. Arthurian legend and the Knights of the Round Table.

**Port Isaac** — 25 miles
Filming location for TV series *Doc Martin*. "Winding streets, charming pubs, independent shops." Home of the Fisherman's Friends sea shanties group.

**Padstow** — just under 1 hour
Fishing port at head of Camel River. Rick Stein's Seafood Restaurant. Camel Cycle Trail.

**Newquay** — (distance not specified)
"One of the UK's most popular seaside towns." Beaches, theme parks, zoos, surf schools, dining. Fistral Beach highlighted.

**Fowey** — just over 1 hour
Fishing port on estuary. Waterfront dining. Ferry to Polruan.

## FAMILY DAYS OUT — /family-days-out

### Heading: "Family Days out"

### Introduction
"With a wide range of stunning beaches, plenty of local landmarks, theme parks, zoos, museums and other activities… Cornwall is the perfect place for a family day out!"

### Featured Attractions

**Local Beaches**
Millook, Widemouth Beach, Summerleaze recommended.

**Local Water Sports**
Bude Canoe Experience — "breathtaking scenery and rare wildlife for all the family." Local surf schools. Fishing. Indoor and outdoor heated swimming pools at Whalesborough.

**Tintagel Castle**
"Just 18 miles away from us at Whalesborough. Easily one of the most popular destinations for families in Cornwall!" Dramatic bridge with clifftop views.

**Camel Creek Adventure Park**
Near Wadebridge. "Rides and activities for all ages plus restaurants, cafes, shops, a meerkat enclosure, and the UK's most advanced 5D theatre!"

**Newquay Zoo**
"Just an hour's drive away from Whalesborough. Over 1,000 of the world's rarest and most endangered animals." Family tickets available.

**Museum of Witchcraft**
Located in Boscastle. "One of the world's largest collections of items relating to witchcraft and magic, with over 3000 objects on show." Open since 1960.

## DAYS WITH YOUR DOG — /days-with-your-dog

### Heading: "Days out with your dog"

### Featured Dog-Friendly Locations

**Black Rock Beach** — Less than 10 minutes from Whalesborough
Dogs welcome year-round. Rocky outcrops, surf waves, rock pools.

**Northcott Mouth Beach** — 5 miles away
Dogs year-round. Sandy/rocky terrain. Natural wildlife surroundings.
"Untouched, but popular stretch of sand and rocky outcrops"

**Millook Haven Beach** — Over 10 minutes away
Dogs year-round. Large pebbles, dramatic cliffs, quiet atmosphere, limited parking.
"Usually quiet due to limited parking and no facilities"

**South West Coast Path** — Couple miles away
630-mile trail. Beaches, hidden coves, fishing villages.
Recommended entry: Millook, Dizzard, Crackington Haven, Boscastle

**Bodmin Moor**
"Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty" — prehistoric sites, valleys, oak trees, rare plants, otters and butterflies

**Dog-Friendly Pubs**
- The Bay View Inn
- The Brendon Arms
- The Coachman's Bar & Grill
